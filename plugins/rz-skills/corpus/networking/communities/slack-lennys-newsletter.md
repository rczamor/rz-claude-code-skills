# Slack — Lenny's Newsletter Community (paid)

## What it is
The paid Slack community attached to Lenny's Newsletter — one of the highest-quality, most curated PM communities on the internet. Subscription-based admission filters for engaged senior PMs and product leaders. High signal, lower volume, and strongly cross-pollinated with the Lenny's podcast and YouTube audience.

## Why it matters
Lenny's community is the closest peer to CPO Club HQ in quality, with broader topical scope (full PM craft, not just C-level concerns). The audience overlaps heavily with Segment 1 and the upper end of Segment 2. Visibility here translates strongly into speaking invitations, advisory inquiries, and Tier 2 → Tier 1 promotions because the community itself functions as a status-signal: being a recognized contributor in Lenny's community accelerates credibility outside it.

## How to apply

Engagement style: **curated, premium, high-quality. Match the community's bar.**

1. Read at minimum 100+ messages before first post. The bar is high; bad first impression is hard to undo.
2. Pick 2 channels to be active in (typically #ai-products and #leadership-discussion). Engage in others sparingly.
3. Every post should be a unit of value — a specific frame, a specific example, a specific lesson. No filler comments, no agreement-only replies.
4. The community values rare insights from real practitioners. Riché's combination of VP of Product context, founder context, and current builder context is exactly what the community rewards.
5. Self-promotion: low but tolerated when tied to specific substance. A "here's the writeup of what I've been working on" link is acceptable when the writeup itself adds value to a discussion.
6. The DM rate is unusually high here — even one strong post in a channel can generate 5+ DMs from senior people. Capacity-plan accordingly.

Watch-out: the community runs heavily on Lenny Rachitsky's voice and aesthetic. Posts that violate the cultural norm (too aggressive, too self-promotional, too thin) get noticed by the founder himself.

## Examples
1. In #ai-products, a senior PM asks for examples of teams that successfully shipped AI features that survived a model migration. Riché posts a real example from advisory work — concrete, multi-layer, attribution-aware. Generates 8 thread replies and 4 DMs, including two from CPOs of named companies.
2. In #leadership-discussion, a question about hiring senior PMs onto AI product teams. Riché's substantive answer cross-pollinates into a Lenny's podcast guest invitation 3 months later. The compounding loop runs through visibility here.
