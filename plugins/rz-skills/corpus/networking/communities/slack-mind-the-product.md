# Slack — Mind the Product / Product-Led Alliance

## What it is
Two of the largest active PM-focused Slack communities — Mind the Product (MTP, ~14,000 members) and Product-Led Alliance (PLA). Both are free, broadly attended by working PMs across seniority levels, and active on a daily basis with a mix of craft questions, role-transition discussions, and AI-product conversations.

## Why it matters
MTP and PLA are where Tier 3 audience growth happens fastest. Active, peer-discussion oriented, with high volume of substantive questions Riché can answer with real expertise. The audience skews mid-senior PM — Segment 2 and Segment 3 in `corpus/growth/segments/` — with occasional Segment 1 leaders. Visibility here doesn't directly produce Tier 1 access, but it builds the recognition base that compounds into speaking invitations and DMs.

## How to apply

Engagement style: **active, peer-discussion focus, post substantive answers.**

1. Lurk for the first week. Read 50+ messages per channel before posting. Map who the regulars are, what topics get discussion, and which channels are active.
2. Pick 2-3 channels to be active in (typically #ai-products, #pm-craft, and one role-discussion channel). Don't try to be in every channel.
3. Answer questions where you have a real opinion, with substance and specifics. Reference Riché's actual experience (Suzy, Grandstage, Helm Labs, IBM) — never generic advice.
4. Follow the 2:1 answer-to-promotion ratio from `corpus/networking/communities/engagement-rules.md`. Self-promotion that lands here looks aggressive elsewhere.
5. DM the people whose questions you answer well. Most Tier 3 → Tier 2 promotions in MTP happen through follow-up DMs, not in-channel exchanges.

Watch-out: MTP and PLA both have anti-self-promotion norms enforced by mods. Drop-and-run posts get removed; substantive posts that happen to mention work get tolerated. Stay on the right side.

## Examples
1. A question in MTP #ai-products: "How are folks handling RAG quality plateaus?" Riché posts a substantive answer with the three-layer context shaping framework, references the failure modes he's instrumented at Grandstage, and ends with "happy to dig deeper if useful." Generates 4 thread replies and 2 DMs over the next week.
2. A role-transition question in PLA: "Anyone made the IC PM → Director jump in an AI-first product team?" Riché shares specific lessons from his Suzy progression, no self-promotion. Builds Tier 3 awareness slowly but durably.
