# Weekly Review Cadence

## What it is
A 15-minute weekly review of the relationship CRM, embedded inside the weekly admin block defined in the growth-marketing weekly playbook. Not a deep planning session — a fast scan of the rows to identify stale next-actions, queue this week's touches, and update tier movements. The cadence is what keeps the CRM from rotting.

## Why it matters
Personal CRMs decay fast without a fixed cadence. The 15-minute weekly slot is short enough to actually happen and frequent enough to keep next-actions fresh. Skipping the review for two weeks turns the CRM into stale data that no longer reflects reality, at which point Riché stops trusting it and stops using it. The cadence is the entire system.

## How to apply

The 15-minute structure:

1. **Tier 1 scan (3 min)** — read every Tier 1 row. Has each person heard from Riché in the past month? Any next-actions overdue? Any signal that someone should drop tier? Queue 1-2 substantive Tier 1 touches for the week.

2. **Tier 2 scan (5 min)** — scan all 30-50 rows. Identify the 3-5 with stale next-actions, the 2-3 due for a periodic touch, and any Tier 3 candidates that should promote up. Queue 5-7 Tier 2 touches for the week.

3. **Tier 3/Tier 4 scan (3 min)** — only the rows with active next-actions. Most of Tier 3 doesn't live in the CRM — it lives in LinkedIn metrics. The rows here are the active ones being worked.

4. **Tier movement (2 min)** — apply `corpus/networking/crm/tier-movement-rules.md` to anything that's earned a promotion or demotion. Don't be reluctant to demote — stale Tier 2 entries that haven't been engaged in 6 months should drop to Tier 3 or Tier 4.

5. **Queue this week's outbound (2 min)** — list the 6-10 specific touches for the week, in writing, with named people and concrete actions.

## When to do it

Embedded in the weekly admin block per the growth-marketing playbook. Same day, same time, every week. Friday afternoons or Monday mornings work well — far enough from the workweek crunch that the brain has slack.

## Examples
1. Friday 4pm: Riché opens the CRM, scans Tier 1 (5 entries), sees one person hasn't heard from him in 6 weeks → queues a substantive DM. Scans Tier 2, identifies 4 stale next-actions → queues touches. Scans Tier 3, 2 entries have engaged on a recent post → promotes both to Tier 2. Total time: 12 minutes. Outbound queue: 7 touches for the next week.
2. Monday morning: Riché executes the queued 7 touches over the course of the week, at maybe 5-10 minutes per touch — total 50 minutes of outreach, fully prepared by the Friday review.

## Anti-patterns
- Skipping the review when the week is busy. The week is always busy; the cadence is what matters.
- Letting the review balloon to 45 minutes. If it's growing, the CRM is too heavy — prune fields and rows.
- Reviewing without queuing concrete outbound. The review's purpose is the queue; without it, the review is just data-staring.
