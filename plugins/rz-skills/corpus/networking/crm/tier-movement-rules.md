# Tier Movement Rules

## What it is
Explicit promotion and demotion rules for moving people between Tier 1, Tier 2, Tier 3, and Tier 4 in the CRM. Without rules, tier assignments calcify and stop reflecting reality. The rules turn relationship momentum into a tracked signal — letting Riché see who is rising into deeper engagement and who is drifting away.

## Why it matters
Most personal CRMs encode a snapshot at one moment in time and never update. Tier movement rules make the CRM a dynamic instrument: it tracks where each relationship is heading, not just where it stands. The rules also force discipline — Riché has to demote relationships that have actually gone dormant, not pretend they're still active.

## How to apply

### Promotion rules (deeper engagement)

- **Tier 3 → Tier 2**: One substantive two-way exchange beyond the initial content engagement. The recipient has commented thoughtfully, DM'd, or had a real conversation; Riché has reciprocated; both parties know each other's name and at least one specific thing about each other's work.
- **Tier 2 → Tier 1**: Three or more substantive exchanges over 2+ months, plus mutual willingness to take a call when needed. Tier 1 promotion is rare and significant — most Tier 2 stays Tier 2.
- **Tier 4 → Tier 2**: Successful reactivation via the dormant-network template, followed by a substantive follow-up exchange. Reactivated dormants typically land in Tier 2, not back in Tier 1, because the relationship needs to be re-warmed.

### Demotion rules (drift away)

- **Tier 1 → Tier 2**: Two months without substantive contact (either direction). Tier 1 requires regular density; without it, the relationship is functionally Tier 2.
- **Tier 2 → Tier 3**: Six months without two-way engagement (DM, comment thread, in-person). They're still aware; they're not actively engaged.
- **Tier 2/Tier 3 → Tier 4**: Twelve months without any engagement. The relationship is now dormant and requires an activation message to revive.

### Special cases

- **Capacity caps**: Tier 1 caps at 10 (per `corpus/networking/tiers/tier-1-inner-circle.md`). If a Tier 2 promotion would push Tier 1 to 11, Riché demotes the lowest-density Tier 1 to Tier 2 first.
- **Tier 4 is permanent** for the underlying relationship history but the activation status is what's tracked. A dormant Techstars contact stays Tier 4 until reactivated; reactivation moves them to Tier 2 or Tier 3 based on the post-reactivation density.

## Examples
1. A Tier 3 contact who reposted Riché's content twice and then DM'd with a specific question about context architecture, where Riché answered and got a substantive follow-up: promoted to Tier 2. Updated in the next weekly review.
2. A Tier 1 mentor whom Riché hasn't actually had a substantive call with in 3 months despite passing pleasantries on LinkedIn: demoted to Tier 2. Honest accounting matters more than aspiration.
3. A Techstars cohort founder Riché hasn't been in touch with for 18 months: confirmed Tier 4, queued for activation message in the next weekly cycle.

## Anti-patterns
- Promoting based on hope rather than evidence. "We had one good chat, this is Tier 1 material" — no, it isn't yet.
- Refusing to demote. Tier rot is the leading cause of CRM uselessness.
