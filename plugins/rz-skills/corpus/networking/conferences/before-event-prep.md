# Before-Event Prep

## What it is
The structured preparation Riché does before any conference, meetup, or networking event — typically 2-4 hours of work in the days leading up to the event. The prep makes the difference between a useful event (3-5 substantive new relationships, multiple follow-ups queued) and a wasted one (business cards in a pile, names already forgotten by the next morning).

## Why it matters
Most attendees show up cold and leave with random contacts. The prepared approach lets Riché walk in with target connections identified, conversation hooks ready, and follow-up infrastructure pre-staged — which compounds across events. ProductTank NYC and similar venues are too crowded to network well by accident; the prep is what makes them productive.

## How to apply
Five-step prep checklist:

1. **Review speaker list and attendee list if available.** Most events publish speakers; some publish attendees. LinkedIn-search the venue or hashtag for who's planning to attend.

2. **Identify 3-5 specific people to connect with and why.** Write the name, role, company, and the specific reason — "Sarah, CPO at [company], launched feature using context-aware UX patterns I want to discuss." If you can't write a specific reason, deprioritize.

3. **Prepare a one-line conversational description** of what Riché does — not a pitch. Canonical line: "I'm building a system that proves context architecture matters more than model intelligence in AI products." See `corpus/networking/conferences/conversational-pitch-line.md`.

4. **Prepare 2-3 questions** that demonstrate genuine curiosity about their work. Generic ("what's your biggest challenge?") fails; specific ("how is your team handling context degradation in long agent loops?") succeeds. See `corpus/networking/conferences/question-templates.md`.

5. **Pre-write the follow-up message templates** before the event — much easier to write when not tired post-event. Have a Cold-LinkedIn template and a warm follow-up template ready to personalize within 48 hours.

## Examples
1. ProductTank NYC, monthly, ~150 attendees: Riché reviews speaker bios, identifies 4 target connects (one CPO, two senior PMs, one founder), pre-writes the conversational pitch line, drafts 2 specific questions per target, and pre-builds a follow-up template that just needs a specific-reference insert.
2. Lean Product Meetup (virtual), ~80 attendees: prep is lighter (no speaker list often), but Riché still queues 3 target connections from the registration page LinkedIn-search, plus question templates that work for the panel format.

## Anti-patterns
- Showing up without target connects. The conversations become reactive instead of intentional.
- Skipping the follow-up pre-write. Post-event fatigue kills 80% of follow-ups; the pre-write defends against it.
- Generic prep questions. They make every conversation feel like an interview, not a peer exchange.
