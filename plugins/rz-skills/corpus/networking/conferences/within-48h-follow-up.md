# Within-48-Hour Follow-Up

## What it is
The post-event protocol Riché executes within 48 hours of any conference or meetup. Four steps: send specific-reference messages, connect on LinkedIn with personalized notes, assign to relationship tier, and deliver any promised shares. The 48-hour window is non-negotiable — past it, the conversations decay in the recipient's memory and the follow-up stops feeling warm.

## Why it matters
The 48-hour window is the highest-leverage moment in the entire networking workflow. The prep was for this; the during-event posture was for this. A single hour of focused follow-up produces more compounding relationship value than ten hours of cold outreach to the same people six months later. Most attendees miss this window — and their event ROI collapses to near-zero.

## How to apply
Four-step protocol, executed within 48 hours:

1. **Send follow-up messages referencing something specific from the conversation.** Use the pre-written templates from before-event-prep, personalized with the specific note from the during-event posture. Reference the exact phrase, problem, or moment from the conversation.

2. **Connect on LinkedIn with a personalized note** — never the default "I'd like to add you." The note can be one sentence: "Great to meet at ProductTank — your line about context budgeting is going to live in my head for a while."

3. **Add to the appropriate relationship tier** in the CRM. Most new event contacts land in Tier 3; a few who had high-substance conversations may land directly in Tier 2. See `corpus/networking/crm/tier-movement-rules.md`.

4. **If promised to share something, share it immediately.** The "I'd love to send you something about X" intent from the contact exchange becomes a real send within the 48-hour window. Late delivery breaks the trust signal the offer created.

## Examples
1. ProductTank NYC follow-up: 4 target connects met. Within 24 hours, Riché sends 4 personalized DMs (each referencing a specific conversation moment), connects on LinkedIn with personalized notes, adds 3 to Tier 3 and 1 to Tier 2 in the CRM, and emails the two writeups he promised. Total time: 60 minutes.
2. Conference talk follow-up: After Riché speaks at an event, 12 attendees approach him after. Within 48 hours, he sends each a personalized DM referencing what they specifically asked or said, connects on LinkedIn, and shares the deck plus the one-page summary. Of 12, typically 6-8 respond and 2-3 enter Tier 2.

## Anti-patterns
- Mass-sending generic follow-ups. The whole value is in the specific reference; without it, mass DMs land worse than no DM.
- Skipping the LinkedIn personalization. Default LinkedIn requests from someone you "met at an event" feel cold even when warm.
- Promising shares and not delivering. The single most common networking failure mode — and it kills the relationship before it starts.
