# Warm Follow-Up After Engagement

## What it is
A direct message sent after a comment-thread exchange or post engagement on LinkedIn. The recipient has already engaged — replied to Riché's comment, liked his post, or commented on his content. This is the conversion message that moves a content interaction into a one-to-one relationship.

## Why it matters
Engagement on a public post is shallow by default. Without a warm follow-up, the interaction evaporates within 48 hours and the recipient resumes anonymity in Tier 3. The warm follow-up is the bridge that promotes Tier 3 to Tier 2 — and it's only effective when sent within the active window (3-7 days after the exchange) while the conversation is still recent in their head.

## How to apply
Structure:
1. Reference the specific exchange — name the post, the comment thread, or the moment.
2. Extend with a new thought or angle that builds on what they said.
3. Specific next step — never "let's chat sometime." Always "would love to swap notes on [specific topic]" or "happy to share the writeup I'm doing on [specific thing]."

Length: 100-150 words. Longer than a cold connect because the relationship is warmer.

Timing: 3-7 days after the engagement. Sooner reads as eager; later reads as stale.

## Examples

Example message — to a CPO who replied to Riché's comment on a third party's post about agent reliability:

> Your reply on Anand's post about agent loop failures — the "context windows aren't memory, they're working memory under pressure" line stuck with me. That's the framing I've been missing in how I describe the Hook talk to event organizers. The piece I'm working on now is a sequel to that idea: when agents fail, they're not forgetting, they're context-flooding. I'd love to swap notes on the failure modes you've been seeing in production. Happy to send the draft of the writeup if useful — would value your reaction before it goes public next month.

Word count: 110. References the specific exchange (Anand's post, the working-memory line), extends with new thought (context-flooding sequel), names a concrete next step (swap notes, share draft).

## Anti-patterns
- Vague references ("loved your comment!") — the specifics make it warm; without them it's cold.
- Asking for a call without offering anything substantive first. The exchange must extend value, not extract it.
- Waiting more than 10 days. The engagement window closes; cold-follow-up rules apply at that point.
