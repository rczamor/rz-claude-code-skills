# Speaker Pitch

## What it is
The email or DM Riché sends to event organizers, conference programmers, and meetup hosts to pitch a speaking slot. Lead with the hook. Mention what makes the talk unique. Include one specific audience takeaway. The pitch is short, structured, and demo-ready — the goal is a fast yes.

## Why it matters
Event organizers receive hundreds of pitches per cycle and have <30 seconds per email. The pitch's job is to make the decision easy. A good pitch contains the hook, the differentiation, the takeaway, the credentials, and the logistics — in a structure that lets the organizer scan and decide. A bad pitch buries the hook, talks about Riché instead of the audience, and asks the organizer to do the work of figuring out the value.

## How to apply
Structure:
1. **Hook**: one sentence that makes the talk's premise undeniable. Lead with this.
2. **Differentiation**: live demo capability, practitioner not theorist, backed by cognitive science.
3. **Audience takeaway**: one specific thing attendees will leave with.
4. **Credentials**: Suzy VP of Product, founded two AI companies (300% growth, $3.25M pipeline), IBM veteran. One line.
5. **Logistics**: available for 20-40 minute slots, can do fireside chat or structured talk.

Total length: 150-250 words. Plus a one-sheet attachment styled per `corpus/brand-system/`.

The hook itself: "I built a personal knowledge engine from scratch to prove that context architecture, not model intelligence, is the real moat in AI products." Canonical hook lives in `corpus/growth/speaking/the-hook.md`.

## Examples

Example pitch — to ProductTank NYC for a 30-minute speaker slot:

> Hi [organizer],
>
> I built a personal knowledge engine from scratch to prove that context architecture, not model intelligence, is the real moat in AI products. I'd like to pitch it as a talk for ProductTank NYC.
>
> The talk is built around live demos: I show the system (Grandstage) in real time, walk through the five steps most AI products skip between data and context, and connect the architecture to cognitive science research on working memory. Audience leaves with a concrete failure-mode checklist they can run on their own AI product the same week.
>
> Background: VP of Product at Suzy, founded two AI companies (300% revenue growth at one, $3.25M pipeline at the other), IBM design veteran. I've spoken at [past venues] on adjacent material.
>
> I can do 20, 30, or 40-minute slots, fireside or structured. Available [date range]. Speaker one-sheet attached.
>
> Riché

Word count: 165. Hook leads. Differentiation, takeaway, credentials, logistics each compressed to one sentence.

## Anti-patterns
- Burying the hook past paragraph two. Organizers stop reading.
- Listing every talk Riché has ever given. Three relevant venues, max.
- Pitching a talk without a live demo angle. Demos are the differentiation; without them the talk is generic AI thought leadership.
