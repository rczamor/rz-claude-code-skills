# Tier 3 — Extended Network

## What it is
Two hundred-plus people aware of Riché's work primarily through content — they see his LinkedIn posts in their feed, they may have liked or briefly engaged once, and they would recognize his name if it came up in conversation. They're not yet engaged enough to refer him cold, but they're warm enough that one substantive exchange could pull them into Tier 2.

## Why it matters
Tier 3 is the leading indicator of audience growth and the recruiting pool for Tier 2. Without a healthy Tier 3, Tier 2 stagnates and the network calcifies. The conversion mechanism: content creates Tier 3, then one meaningful interaction (a real comment, a useful DM, a conference handshake) promotes a subset to Tier 2. Most networking effort wasted on Tier 3 is wasted because there's no clear promotion mechanic.

## How to apply
1. Track approximate Tier 3 size via LinkedIn followers + post impressions, not name lists. The volume here is too high to manage manually.
2. Treat one strong piece of content per week as the primary Tier 3 maintenance — the read-then-recognize signal.
3. When a Tier 3 contact engages substantively (a comment with real substance, a thoughtful DM), respond personally and consider promotion to Tier 2.
4. Don't try to convert all of Tier 3. Convert the 5-10% who are Segment 1-aligned and showing engagement signal.

## Examples
1. A senior PM at a Forbes AI 50 company who has reposted three of Riché's posts but never DM'd. They're Tier 3 today; one substantive comment from Riché on their next post is the promotion mechanism.
2. ProductTank NYC attendees who saw Riché speak six months ago. They remember the talk. A targeted "here's the deck plus three follow-up reads" email moves a subset into Tier 2.

## Anti-patterns
- Counting Tier 3 as engagement when it's only impressions. Awareness without exchange is not relationship.
- DM'ing Tier 3 cold with Tier 1 asks. Mismatch in temperature — they don't know him well enough to take the meeting.
