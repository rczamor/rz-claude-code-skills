# Tier 1 — Inner Circle

## What it is
Five to ten people in deep mutual-mentorship relationships with Riché. They take his calls today, he takes theirs. Examples include a few CPO peers, founder-mentors from Techstars and Antler, and one or two senior product leaders he trades work-in-progress with. These relationships get personal attention — never templated outreach, never automated reminders, never "let me know if I can help" filler.

## Why it matters
Tier 1 is where career-shaping decisions get pressure-tested before they happen. Job moves, founder bets, advisory commitments, content positioning — all run through this circle first. A weak Tier 1 produces lonely decision-making and slow career compounding. A strong Tier 1 produces real-time edits to Riché's strategy and the warm intros that cannot be manufactured cold.

## How to apply
1. Cap at 10. Going wider dilutes the depth — better to keep the bench narrow and the relationship density high.
2. Touch each person at least once a month with substantive content (a real question, a reaction to their work, a pass-through of something useful), not just check-ins.
3. Ship value before asking for it — share a relevant intro, a frame, a piece of work — then the asks land naturally.
4. Maintain a one-line note per person on what they're currently obsessed with, so every touch is contextually live.

## Examples
1. A Suzy-era VP of Product who reviews Riché's positioning before public moves; Riché in turn pressure-tests her hiring rubrics and AI tooling decisions monthly.
2. A founder from the Techstars cohort building in adjacent infrastructure; quarterly working sessions where each presents a hard problem to the other, no pitch deck, no agenda.

## Anti-patterns
- Treating Tier 1 like a status badge. People feel when they're being collected vs. genuinely engaged.
- Letting Tier 1 atrophy because the relationship feels safe. Inner-circle decay is invisible until you need a call returned and it isn't.
