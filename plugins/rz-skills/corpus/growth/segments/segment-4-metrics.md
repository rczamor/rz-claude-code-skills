# Segment 4 Metrics

## What it is
The four leading-indicator proxies for Segment 4 (Event Organizers and Podcast Hosts) reach:

1. **Speaking invitations (inbound)** — organizers reaching out without prior pitch.
2. **CFP acceptances** — outbound CFP submissions that convert to accepted talks.
3. **Podcast guest requests** — podcast hosts or bookers reaching out (or accepting outbound pitches).
4. **Event organizer DMs** — connection requests, intro DMs, or "we should chat" messages from event organizers.

## Why it matters
Segment 4 proxies are the most binary of any segment — invitations and acceptances are concrete events, easy to count, and tied directly to high-leverage opportunities. Quality of proxy matters: a Lenny's Podcast booking is worth more than 10 mid-tier event invitations. The dashboard should distinguish target-tier events from secondary ones.

## How to apply
- Track quarterly: speaking invitations received (inbound vs. outbound conversion), CFP submissions and acceptance rate, podcast pitches and conversion rate, event organizer DMs.
- Maintain a target list of priority events and podcasts (see `corpus/growth/speaking/target-events.md` and `corpus/growth/speaking/target-podcasts.md`). Track conversion against the priority tier, not just total count.
- Review CFP outcomes for pattern. Which hooks landed? Which angles got rejected? Adjust the hook library.
- Correlate Segment 4 inbound with Segment 2 reshare patterns from prior quarter. Strong peer reshare in Q1 typically predicts Segment 4 inbound in Q2.

## Examples
- Q1 2026: 2 inbound speaking invitations (ProductTank NYC, an internal AI product summit), 3 CFP submissions with 1 acceptance (AI Product Summit), 1 podcast booking (mid-tier PM podcast), 4 event organizer DMs after the Wednesday LinkedIn deep dive on context architecture.
- The ProductTank NYC invitation traces back to a Segment 2 peer who reshared Riché's deep dive and recommended him to the organizer.
- A CFP rejection to a major conference is logged with the rejection reason. The hook is adjusted; the next CFP cycle uses the refined version.

## Anti-patterns
- Counting every speaking invitation as equal. A Lenny's Podcast booking is worth more than 5 mid-tier invitations; weight the metrics.
- Treating CFP rejection as silence. Each rejection is information; log the reason and refine the hook.
