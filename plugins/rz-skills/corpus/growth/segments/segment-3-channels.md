# Segment 3 Channels

## What it is
Where Segment 3 (Founders Building AI Products) actually pays attention. The pattern: X primary (not LinkedIn), YC forums and Hacker News for technical depth, accelerator-specific Slacks (Techstars, Antler, YC), founder-curated communities. Founders read X threads, click code-heavy bios, and trust GitHub-visible work.

## Why it matters
Channel mismatch is the dominant failure mode here. PMs are a LinkedIn audience; founders are an X audience. The same content needs to be adapted (5–7 tweet thread on Tuesday vs. carousel deep dive on Wednesday). Treating LinkedIn as the only channel will systematically miss this segment, and Segment 3 advisory inquiries will stay near zero.

## How to apply
- **X (primary):** repurpose Wednesday LinkedIn deep dives as 5–7 tweet threads. Engage with the 9 X target accounts. Quote-tweet with added perspective.
- **YC forums and Hacker News:** participate where genuine; don't astroturf. The community sniffs marketing instantly.
- **Accelerator alumni Slacks:** Techstars and Antler where Riché has standing. Substantive participation in technical threads.
- **Founder Slacks (private, invite-only):** participate when invited; don't chase invitations. These are high-trust, low-volume environments.
- **Don't bother with:** LinkedIn-only effort, Threads, Latent Space (engineering-focused, not founder-focused).

## Examples
- Riché's Wednesday LinkedIn deep dive on context architecture is adapted Tuesday into a 6-tweet X thread. The thread is quote-tweeted by a Series A founder; that pulls 3 founder DMs.
- An Antler founder mentions Riché's framework in the accelerator Slack. Riché doesn't see it directly but learns about it through a connection. Two founders DM after.
- A Hacker News thread on retrieval architecture gets a substantive Riché reply (with the live demo URL). The reply ranks high; one founder reads, DMs.

## Anti-patterns
- Posting LinkedIn carousels and expecting founders to find them. Format and platform mismatch.
- Cold-DM'ing YC founders. The community treats cold pitches as noise; warm intros via accelerator Slacks compound.
