# Segment 1 Channels

## What it is
Where Segment 1 (AI Product Hiring Managers) actually pays attention. They do not consume newsletters, deep-dive articles, or thread takes. They scan LinkedIn between meetings — usually the home feed, sometimes a search for a candidate name, occasionally a peer's profile. They trust peer recommendations spoken in private channels (text, DMs, executive Slacks).

## Why it matters
Channel choice is downstream of audience behavior. If you write for "everyone interested in AI product," you produce content that doesn't fit how Segment 1 reads. If you write for Segment 2 *with* Segment 1 lurking, the channel selection is automatic: LinkedIn primary, with a profile and pinned posts that hold up to a 30-second scan.

## How to apply
- **LinkedIn home feed (primary):** posts that show up there because Segment 2 peers reshared. This is the dominant Segment 1 surface.
- **LinkedIn profile (conversion surface):** when Segment 1 lands on the profile (curious about a name they saw), the headline, About, and pinned content do the work. Optimize for that.
- **Peer recommendations (highest-trust):** Segment 1 hires through their network. Build with Segment 2 leaders who are one degree from your target Segment 1 list.
- **Speaking stages (high-leverage):** when a Segment 1 leader is in the audience at ProductTank or a CPO Summit, the talk does what no post can.

## Examples
- A VP of Product at an AI company doesn't follow Riché but follows Lenny Rachitsky. Lenny reshares Riché's deep dive on context architecture. The VP scrolls past once, again, the third time clicks the profile. That sequence is Segment 1's actual channel pattern.
- At ProductTank NYC, a CPO from a Forbes AI 50 company is in the audience. After Riché's talk on context architecture, she texts a peer, "Have you met this guy?" That's Segment 1's private channel.

## Anti-patterns
- Spending time on Threads, Medium, or newsletters thinking "more channels = more reach." Segment 1 is on LinkedIn and at conferences, not on Threads.
- Optimizing the profile for "everyone" — generic PM headlines lose Segment 1's 30-second scan. Lead with differentiation.
