# Segment 1 — AI Product Hiring Managers

## What it is
The smallest, highest-stakes audience: roughly 500–1,000 VP- and CPO-level product leaders at AI companies who hire senior PMs into the roles Riché wants. These are the people who, if they trust him, will pull him into a Series A/B founding-PM seat or a head-of-product role. Forbes AI 50 leadership, post-Series A AI product orgs, big-tech AI product VPs.

## Why it matters
Segment 1 is the conversion target for Riché's career — the peer-trust flywheel exists to make him visible to this segment. But Segment 1 does not consume content. They scan LinkedIn between meetings, trust peer referrals, and rely on their network to surface candidates. Trying to "reach" them with a thread or a clever post is a category error. They're reached indirectly, through Segments 2 and 3 amplifying his work.

## How to apply
- Treat Segment 1 as a *destination*, not an *audience*. Never write content "for" them.
- Optimize the LinkedIn profile, website, and speaker one-sheet for the moment a Segment 1 leader does land on the page (pulled in by a peer reshare or referral).
- Track Segment 1 reach indirectly via the proxies in `corpus/growth/segments/segment-1-metrics.md` (recruiter DMs, profile views from AI company employees, "how did you hear about me" traces).
- Refuse to dilute messaging to flatter Segment 1 generalists. The differentiation is what Segment 2 reshares, not what Segment 1 already agrees with.

## Examples
- A VP of Product at an AI company sees Lenny Rachitsky reshare Riché's deep dive on context architecture. She doesn't comment, but two weeks later her recruiter sends Riché a DM. Segment 1 was reached through Segment 2.
- A CPO at a Forbes AI 50 company gets a peer recommendation: "You should look at Riché Zamor — he's the context architecture guy." That recommendation came from a Segment 2 connection Riché had been engaging with for a quarter.

## Anti-patterns
- Writing posts that flatter VPs/CPOs ("here's what every CPO should know about AI") — this signals candidate, not peer; Segment 1 sees through it.
- Cold-DM'ing hiring managers with the latest essay. They didn't ask, the warmth is fake, and the cost is reputational.
