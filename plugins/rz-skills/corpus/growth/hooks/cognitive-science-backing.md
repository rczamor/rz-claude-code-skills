# Cognitive Science Backing

## What it is
The third differentiation pillar: Riché's framework for context architecture is grounded in information theory and cognitive science — not vibes, not "AI feels different." Specifically: Shannon information theory, Kahneman's dual-process theory, working-memory constraints, retrieval cue research. The substance is academic; the application is practical.

## Why it matters
The AI/PM space is full of vibes-based commentary. "AI is different because it's intelligent" — vague, ungrounded, doesn't survive a follow-up question from a Segment 1 CPO. Cognitive science backing makes the position survive scrutiny: when Riché says "context architecture is the moat," he can point to the information-theoretic reason and the cognitive constraints that make it true. Substance compounds; vibes don't.

## How to apply
- **Reference the underlying academic frame in deep dives.** Wednesday content type pieces should include the substance — Shannon, Kahneman, working-memory research — not just the practical conclusion.
- **Don't over-academicize.** The audience is PMs and founders, not researchers. The pattern: ground the position in 1–2 sentences of academic frame, then translate immediately to product implication.
- **Use the cognitive science angle for technical/founder audiences.** Latent Space-adjacent or AI Product Summit pitches lean harder on this pillar.
- **Pair with practitioner credentials.** Cognitive science alone reads as theorist; practitioner alone reads as ungrounded. The bundle is the differentiation. See `corpus/growth/speaking/differentiation.md`.

## Examples
- A Wednesday deep dive on context architecture: "Shannon's information theory says: the user's intent is conditional on the context they're operating from. AI products that ignore the context layer are operating with a degraded conditional probability — the model doesn't know what the user knows. Working-memory research adds: humans can hold ~4 items in active context; the system must externalize the rest. Most AI PMs treat this as a model problem; it's an information-theoretic problem."
- A speaker one-sheet bio: "Practitioner with cognitive science backing. The Context Layer Engine implements Shannon-style conditional structure plus dual-process retrieval grounded in Kahneman's frame."
- A podcast interview: when asked "why context architecture?" Riché answers with the 30-second cognitive science version, then immediately illustrates with the Suzy launch context drift moment. Substance + practitioner.
