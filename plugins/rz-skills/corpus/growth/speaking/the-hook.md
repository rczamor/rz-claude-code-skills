# The Speaker Hook

## What it is
The differentiated speaker hook — the one-sentence pitch that opens every Segment 4 outreach (event organizer DMs, CFP submissions, podcast pitches, speaker one-sheets):

> "I built a personal knowledge engine from scratch to prove that context architecture, not model intelligence, is the real moat in AI products. I can demo it live on stage."

## Why it matters
Most AI/PM speaker pitches sound the same. "I'm a senior PM who's worked on AI products and would love to share lessons." Generic, defensible, ignored. The hook above is none of those things — it's a sharp position (context architecture > model intelligence), backed by a specific artifact (a personal knowledge engine), with a unique on-stage offer (live demo). It's the single sentence that does the most work in the entire growth system.

## How to apply
- **Open every Segment 4 outreach with this hook.** LinkedIn DMs to organizers, CFP abstracts, podcast pitch emails — see `corpus/networking/outreach/speaker-pitch.md` for full templates.
- **Adapt the close to the audience:**
  - For Tier 1 PM events (Lenny's, Mind the Product): keep as-is, audience already understands "moat" and "context architecture."
  - For mainstream business events (CPO Summit, internal corporate): add a 1-line clarifier — "Most AI products invest in better models; the breakthrough teams invest in better context."
  - For technical/founder audiences (AI Product Summit, Latent Space): lead with technical specifics — "I built the retrieval architecture, evals, and the five-step diagnostic from scratch."
- **The "live demo" is non-negotiable.** Don't drop it for events that don't typically have demos. The live demo is the differentiator.
- **Pair with credentials.** Helm Labs $3.25M pipeline; Suzy 350+ enterprise customers; IBM background. The hook is the position; the credentials are the proof.

## Examples
- LinkedIn DM to ProductTank NYC organizer: opens with the hook verbatim. Closes with "ProductTank NYC's audience is exactly the senior PM peer group I've been engaging with on LinkedIn — happy to demo the Context Layer Engine live for them." Booked.
- CFP submission to AI Product Summit: hook is the abstract opener. Three audience takeaways follow. Credentials in the bio. Accepted.
- Podcast pitch to Lenny's Podcast: hook + "your audience has been resharing my context-architecture deep dives; the live-demo angle would be a natural fit." Plus suggested format (45-min interview with optional live demo segment).

## Anti-patterns
- Softening the hook to be more "professional." "I have some thoughts on AI PMing" is the wrong direction. Sharpness is the differentiator.
- Pitching events without the demo. The live demo is what makes this hook work.
