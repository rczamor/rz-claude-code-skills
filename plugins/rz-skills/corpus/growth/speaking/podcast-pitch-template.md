# Podcast Pitch Template

## What it is
The structure for podcast guest pitches. Four parts: hook + specific angle for that show + proof point + suggested format. Differs from CFP template (which has 5 sections) because podcasts need a more conversational opener and an explicit format suggestion.

## Why it matters
Podcast bookers read dozens of pitches a week. The good ones land because they're tailored — they tell the booker what makes this guest interesting *for this specific show* and reduce the booker's friction by suggesting a format. Generic "I'd love to be on the show" pitches are deleted.

## How to apply

**1. Hook (1–2 sentences).**
The speaker hook (`corpus/growth/speaking/the-hook.md`). Not "I'm an AI PM" — the differentiated hook.

**2. Specific angle for that show (2–3 sentences).**
Why does this guest fit *this* podcast? Reference the host's recent episodes or recurring themes. Example for Lenny's: "Your recent episodes on AI PM craft and the 'why most teams fail' pattern map directly to my framework — context architecture is the layer those failures share."

**3. Proof point (1–2 sentences).**
A specific metric or credential that backs the hook. Helm Labs $3.25M pipeline; Suzy 350+ enterprise customers; IBM background. Pick the one that's most credible for this audience.

**4. Suggested format (1–2 sentences).**
Show the booker the path. "Suggest 45-min interview with optional 10-min live demo segment of the Context Layer Engine — happy to send a 5-min preview clip if useful." Reduces booker work.

**Total length: 100–150 words. Don't pad.**

## Examples

**To Lenny's Podcast booker:**
> "I built a personal knowledge engine from scratch to prove that context architecture, not model intelligence, is the real moat in AI products. I can demo it live during the interview.
>
> Your recent episodes on AI PM craft and 'why most teams fail at AI products' map directly to this — context architecture is the layer those failures share. None of your guests so far have shown the diagnostic on stage; I'd be the first to demo it.
>
> Background: $3.25M pipeline at Helm Labs, 350+ enterprise customers at Suzy, IBM AI Research. I've been a peer in your audience for two years.
>
> Suggested format: 45-min interview with optional 10-min live demo segment of the Context Layer Engine. Happy to send a 5-min preview clip."

**To Product Thinking (Melissa Perri):**
> "I built a personal knowledge engine from scratch to prove that context architecture, not model intelligence, is the real moat in AI products. Most AI PMs are diagnosing the wrong failure mode.
>
> Your recent leadership episodes touched on org-design tensions for AI teams; my practitioner POV adds: the org tension is downstream of the architecture. Building the right team starts with seeing the right layer.
>
> Background: practitioner — $3.25M pipeline at Helm Labs, 350+ enterprise customers at Suzy.
>
> Suggested format: 60-min leadership interview, with a 10-min walk-through of the Context Layer Engine if helpful for visual segments."

## Anti-patterns
- Generic "would love to be on the show" pitches. Bookers delete on sight.
- Pitching without referencing the host's actual recent episodes. Signals you don't listen to the show.
