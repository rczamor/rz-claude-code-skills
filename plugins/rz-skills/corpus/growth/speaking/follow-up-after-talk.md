# Follow-Up After A Talk

## What it is
The within-48-hour workflow after delivering a talk. Three follow-ups: (1) thank-you to the event organizer, (2) replies to audience members who DM'd, (3) request the recording if not already self-captured. The follow-up window is short — 48 hours — because relationship momentum decays fast.

## Why it matters
The talk itself is half the leverage. The other half is the follow-up that converts attendees into peers, organizers into repeat bookers, and recordings into derivative content. Skipping the follow-up window leaves all that leverage on the table. A talk delivered without follow-up is a 200-person event; a talk delivered with follow-up is the start of a year of compounding.

## How to apply

**Within 24 hours:**
- DM the event organizer with a specific thank-you (reference what worked, what their audience asked, what you'd refine). Not generic.
- Reply to every audience member who DM'd during or right after the talk. Same-day if possible. Brief, specific.
- Capture the recording — request from the organizer if not self-captured.

**Within 48 hours:**
- Send a LinkedIn post recap (Friday story content type if the talk was earlier in the week). Reference the audience and a specific moment.
- Pull 1–2 derivative quotes/clips for the next week's batch.
- Add new connections to the relationship CRM (`corpus/networking/`) with the source tagged ("ProductTank NYC June 22").

**Within 7 days:**
- Schedule the next 4 weeks of derivative content from the recording.
- Pitch 1–2 adjacent events with the recording link as proof.

## Examples
- After ProductTank NYC: same-day DM to organizer ("Audience question on the 5-step diagnostic was exactly what I'd hoped — sharpened my framing for next time"). Within 24h: replies to 4 audience DMs. Within 48h: Friday LinkedIn post on the talk's standout moment. Within 7 days: 4 derivative posts scheduled, 2 podcast pitches sent referencing the recording.
- A talk goes well but the recording is fuzzy. Riché requests a clean copy from the organizer within 24 hours. Recording arrives by day 3; clip workflow starts immediately.
- An audience member DMs during the talk asking about the live demo URL. Same-day reply with the URL plus a specific framing for their context.

## Anti-patterns
- Skipping follow-ups because "the talk went well." That's exactly when the leverage compounds; don't waste it.
- Sending a generic "thanks for having me" to the organizer. Specific feedback is what gets you re-invited.
