# The Engagement Flywheel — Overview

## What it is
The audience-development flywheel that connects every channel and segment into a self-reinforcing loop. The full cycle:

> Content (LinkedIn posts, articles) attracts Segment 2 peers and Segment 3 founders → peers reshare and comment → content reaches Segment 1 hiring managers passively → peers recommend Riché to Segment 4 event organizers → speaking creates recorded content → recorded content fuels more posts → more peers discover → cycle accelerates.

## Why it matters
Each component in isolation produces little. Posts without peer reshares stay in an echo chamber. Speaking without recorded clips doesn't compound. Comments without consistent posting don't convert. The flywheel is the mechanism that turns every individual activity into compounding leverage. Understanding the whole loop prevents over-investing in any single tactic and under-investing in the connective tissue (Segment 2 relationships, recorded content reuse).

## How to apply
- Audit weekly: which stage of the flywheel is bottlenecking? Common patterns:
  - Posts but no reshares → Segment 2 relationship gap (see `corpus/growth/flywheel/starts-with-relationships.md`)
  - Reshares but no Segment 1 traction → time lag is normal; 4–8 weeks
  - Speaking but no derivative posts → recording-to-content workflow missing (see `corpus/growth/flywheel/recorded-content-fuel.md`)
- Optimize for the slowest stage first. Adding more posts when the bottleneck is reshares is wasted effort.
- Track at the cycle level, not just at each tactic. Quarterly review: how many full cycles ran (post → reshare → S1 reach → speaking offer)?

## Examples
- A Wednesday LinkedIn deep dive on context architecture is reshared by 3 senior PMs (Segment 2). The reshares hit a VP of Product's feed (Segment 1) — she views the profile but doesn't engage. Two weeks later, a peer recommends Riché to ProductTank NYC organizer (Segment 4). Riché speaks; the talk produces 6 video clips; one clip becomes the next Wednesday's post. Cycle complete.
- Helm Labs $3.25M pipeline credibility marker shows up in the speaker one-sheet, in pinned LinkedIn posts, and in CFP submissions. The same proof point fuels Segment 2 trust, Segment 3 advisory inquiries, and Segment 4 booking conversion.

## Anti-patterns
- Treating each tactic as standalone (post performance, reshare count, speaking ROI). The flywheel only compounds if the connections work.
- Investing in content volume when the bottleneck is peer relationships. Doubling posts won't fix a Segment 2 gap.
