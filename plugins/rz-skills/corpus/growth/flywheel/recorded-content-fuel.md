# Recorded Content Fuels The Flywheel

## What it is
The reuse loop that turns one stage appearance into 6+ months of content. A 30-minute talk produces 6–10 short clips, 2–3 deep-dive blog posts, 4–5 LinkedIn posts, 2 X threads, and a podcast-ready audio file. Each derivative pulls more peers into the flywheel, which produces more speaking offers, which produces more recorded content.

## Why it matters
Speaking is high-effort, high-reward, but only if the recording is captured and reused. A talk delivered to 200 people in a room is a 200-person event. A talk recorded, clipped, and turned into LinkedIn posts is a 20,000-person event over 6 months. The leverage multiple is 100x — and it's the difference between speaking being a luxury and being the highest-ROI activity per hour.

## How to apply
- **Always record.** Bring your own audio recorder if the venue won't. The recording is the asset.
- **Clip aggressively.** A 30-minute talk yields 6–10 standalone clips of 30–90 seconds each. Each clip is one LinkedIn post.
- **Repurpose into multiple formats:** clips for LinkedIn, threads for X, deep-dive articles for richezamor.com, audio for any podcast pitch.
- **Schedule the derivatives.** Within 7 days of the talk, schedule the next 4 weeks of derivative posts. Don't let recordings sit unused.
- **Reference the recording in pitches.** Future event organizers and podcast hosts can preview the talk; conversion accelerates.

## Examples
- ProductTank NYC talk on context architecture (June 22): 30-minute talk recorded. The recording becomes:
  - 8 LinkedIn clips over 8 weeks (one Wednesday deep-dive replaced with a clip post; new posts added)
  - A 6-tweet X thread
  - A long-form article migrated to richezamor.com (SEO target: "context architecture")
  - Audio sent in podcast pitches as a preview (Lenny's, Product Thinking, etc.)
- AI Product Summit talk produces clips that get reshared by 3 senior PMs. Two of those reshares pull new ProductTank organizer DMs. Cycle reinforces.

## Anti-patterns
- Speaking without recording. The marginal cost of recording is near zero; the cost of not recording is the entire compounding loop.
- Letting recordings sit untouched for months. The half-life of "fresh content" matters; clip and schedule within 2 weeks.
