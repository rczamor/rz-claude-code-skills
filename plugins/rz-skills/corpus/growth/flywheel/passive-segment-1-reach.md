# Passive Segment 1 Reach

## What it is
The mechanism by which Segment 1 (hiring managers) actually encounters Riché's work: not by reading content, but by passively scrolling past peer-reshared posts in their LinkedIn home feed. They don't follow Riché. They don't engage. They scan. And when they land on his profile (because a peer's reshare made them curious), the profile and pinned content do the conversion work.

## Why it matters
This is the "one weird trick" of personal-brand strategy that contradicts most advice. Segment 1 is not an audience to write for — they're a *destination* to optimize for. Designing content to be reshareable by Segment 2 is what reaches Segment 1. Designing content to be readable by Segment 1 directly produces candidate-voice content that fails with both segments.

## How to apply
- **Write for Segment 2.** The content style, voice, and substance should land for senior PM peers. Segment 1 will see what peers have validated.
- **Optimize the LinkedIn profile and pinned content for the 30-second scan.** Headline, About, and pinned posts must lead with differentiation: practitioner, live demo, specific metrics. See `corpus/growth/channels/linkedin-profile-optimization.md`.
- **Lead with practitioner-not-theorist credibility markers.** Helm Labs $3.25M pipeline, Suzy launch with 350+ enterprise customers, IBM background. Not generic PM bona fides.
- **Track Segment 1 reach indirectly via the proxies in `corpus/growth/segments/segment-1-metrics.md`.** Profile views from AI company employees, recruiter DMs, "how did you hear about me" traces.
- **Don't try to hold Segment 1's attention with content.** Their attention budget for any given post is ~8 seconds; the profile is where conversion happens.

## Examples
- A VP of Product at an AI company sees a Wednesday deep dive reshared by Lenny Rachitsky. She doesn't engage. The next week, a similar post from Riché appears in her feed (peer #2 reshared). The third week, she clicks the profile. Within 90 seconds, the practitioner-not-theorist hook + Helm Labs metrics + live demo URL converts curiosity to a recruiter DM.
- A CPO at a Forbes AI 50 company never sees Riché's posts in his feed but hears the name twice in two weeks — once from a peer recommendation, once from a CFP committee mention. He searches "Riché Zamor" on LinkedIn. The profile is the entire conversion surface.

## Anti-patterns
- Writing posts directly for VPs/CPOs. Generic "what every CPO needs to know about AI" content is invisible to Segment 1 and embarrassing to Segment 2.
- Neglecting the profile because "the posts will do the work." The profile *is* the conversion surface for Segment 1.
