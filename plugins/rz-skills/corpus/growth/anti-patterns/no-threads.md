# Don't Publish on Threads

## What it is
The rule: don't invest time in Meta's Threads platform. Threads has shallow professional usage, a consumer-skewed audience, and weak overlap with Riché's segments (PMs are on LinkedIn, founders are on X, hiring managers don't engage on social media at all).

## Why it matters
Threads is the kind of platform that produces FOMO without ROI. It looks active because Instagram's user base is huge; the active professional/PM audience is thin. Spending the daily 15 min on Threads produces near-zero Segment 2/3/4 reach. Honoring the time budget means refusing Threads even when peers say "I'm seeing some pickup there."

## How to apply
- **Don't post on Threads.** No original content, no cross-posts.
- **Use X for the X-native audience** — see `corpus/growth/channels/x-thread-format.md`. That's where founders and Segment 3 actually are.
- **When asked "are you on Threads?" answer honestly:** "No — my audience (senior PMs, AI founders, event organizers) is on LinkedIn and X. Threads doesn't fit."
- **Re-evaluate quarterly.** If Threads' professional audience grew dramatically, the rule could change. Currently, no signal to reconsider.

## Examples
- A peer says "I've been getting some traction on Threads, you should try it." Riché replies: "What's the audience profile? My segments are PM peers and AI founders — Threads doesn't seem to have that density yet." Decision: don't add. 
- A Threads user @-mentions Riché. Riché responds substantively from his profile (engagement is fine when it lands), but doesn't open a Threads cross-posting habit.
- Quarterly review: Threads usage among target accounts (the 18 LinkedIn list + 9 X list)? ~2 of 27 are active on Threads. Density too low; rule holds.

## Anti-patterns
- Adding Threads "to test." Tests cost time; without a clear hypothesis, default to refusing.
- Cross-posting from X to Threads automatically. Different format, different audience norms; auto-cross-post produces awkward content.
