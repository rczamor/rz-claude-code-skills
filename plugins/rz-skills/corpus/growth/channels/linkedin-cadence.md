# LinkedIn Posting Cadence (3–5x/week)

## What it is
The minimum-viable LinkedIn posting cadence: 3–5 posts per week, using the Mon-Fri content types. Monday hot take, Tuesday signal, Wednesday deep dive, Thursday framework, Friday story. The full content type canon lives in `corpus/content-system/`. This entry covers the *cadence* — how often, how distributed across the week, and the algorithm patterns.

## Why it matters
Cadence is the LinkedIn algorithm's primary input. Sub-3 posts/week, the algorithm de-prioritizes the account. 5+ posts/week, returns diminish (and the time budget breaks). 3–5 is the sweet spot. The Mon-Fri distribution also serves the 5-content-type structure — each day has a defined type, which trains the audience and the algorithm both.

## How to apply
- **Post 3–5x per week.** Weekly minimum: Monday, Wednesday, Friday. Stretch: add Tuesday and Thursday for 5x.
- **Use the Mon-Fri content types.** Each day a defined type — see `corpus/content-system/` for hot take / signal / deep dive / framework / story.
- **Best posting time: morning (8–10 AM ET).** When senior PMs are scrolling LinkedIn over coffee. Empirically the highest engagement window for Segment 2.
- **Engage in comments before AND after posting.** See `corpus/growth/channels/linkedin-comment-sandwiching.md` — the algorithm rewards active users.
- **Flex on weeks with talks or major events.** A ProductTank week may run 4 posts (3 normal + 1 talk-derivative); a quiet week stays at 3.

## Examples
- Standard week: Monday hot take on AI PM job descriptions, Wednesday deep dive on context architecture, Friday story on Suzy launch context drift. 3 posts; tight cadence.
- Talk week: same 3 posts, plus Tuesday signal post quoting the upcoming ProductTank talk's framework, plus Thursday talk recap clip. 5 posts; talk-amplified.
- Quiet week (vacation, busy PM week): 3 posts holds. Don't push to 5 if it forces lower-quality content.

## Anti-patterns
- Daily posting. The marginal post past 5 cannibalizes the prior posts' reach and dilutes signal.
- Posting at midnight or weekends thinking "nobody else is." Senior PMs aren't scrolling LinkedIn on Saturday at 11 PM either.
