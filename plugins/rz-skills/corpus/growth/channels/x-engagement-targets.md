# X Engagement Targets (9 Accounts)

## What it is
The 9 X accounts that anchor Riché's X engagement: @shreaborhade, @lennysan, @joulee, @johncutlefish, @sachinrekhi, @caborojo, @gibsonbiddle, @lissijean, @ttorres. The structured version with focus and post archetypes lives in `corpus/growth/target-accounts/x.yaml`. This entry is the rationale and engagement pattern.

## Why it matters
The 15 min/day X block is bounded; without a target list, the time goes to whatever shows up in the feed. The 9-account list ensures every X minute is spent in proximity to PM thinkers whose audience overlaps Segments 2 and 3. Engaging consistently with this set produces both Segment 2 resonance (PM peers) and Segment 3 reach (founders in their orbits).

## How to apply
- **Daily rotation:** in 15 min, scan the 9 accounts' recent tweets. Pick 1–2 to quote-tweet with perspective. Pick 1 to substantively reply to.
- **Match the post archetype.** Each account has a typical post pattern (frameworks, reactions, threads). Match your engagement to the archetype — don't hijack with off-topic.
- **Build on substance.** Same substance rule — extend, refine, name tension, add proof. See `corpus/growth/channels/x-quote-tweet-pattern.md`.
- **Don't engage all 9 every day.** 1–2 substantive engagements > 9 thin ones.
- **Track which engagements pull replies.** Pattern reveals which voice + target combinations work best.

The 9 accounts:

- **@shreaborhade** — PM craft, frameworks. Threads or reactions.
- **@lennysan** — PM newsletter cross-posts, reactions.
- **@joulee** (Julie Zhuo) — design + product leadership; threads.
- **@johncutlefish** (John Cutler) — team metrics, product operating models.
- **@sachinrekhi** — product strategy, cross-functional product.
- **@caborojo** (Carlos Borojo / Aakash) — AI PM specifically.
- **@gibsonbiddle** — Netflix-era PM lessons; frameworks.
- **@lissijean** (Melissa Perri) — product leadership, podcast cross-posts.
- **@ttorres** (Teresa Torres) — discovery, opportunity solution trees.

## Examples
- Shreyas tweets a sharp take on PM frameworks. Riché quote-tweets: "What's missing for AI PMs is that 'framework selection' changes when context architecture changes." Quote-tweet pulls a reply from @caborojo.
- Teresa Torres posts an opportunity solution tree thread. Riché replies substantively in-thread, not as a quote-tweet (because the engagement is with her audience, not with redirecting his own). Two senior PMs reply.
- Daily rotation: Mon @shreaborhade, Tue @ttorres, Wed @joulee + @lennysan, Thu @johncutlefish, Fri @gibsonbiddle. Cover the set across the week without spamming any single account.

## Anti-patterns
- Engaging only the biggest accounts (Lenny, Shreyas). Spread the rotation; smaller accounts often produce stronger relationship returns.
- Pasting the same quote-tweet take across multiple accounts. Each account has a specific archetype; tailor.
