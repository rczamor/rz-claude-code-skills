# Context Layer Engine Demo (Signature Asset)

## What it is
The interactive demo of the Context Layer Engine on richezamor.com — an in-browser walkthrough of the five-step context architecture process Riché built from scratch. Distinct from a video; the demo is interactive and runs in the visitor's browser. This is the single most differentiated asset in the entire growth system.

## Why it matters
Most personal-brand sites have a bio, a blog, and a contact form. The Context Layer Engine demo is what makes Riché's site memorable — and what backs up the speaker hook ("I built a personal knowledge engine from scratch... I can demo it live on stage"). When a Segment 4 organizer or a Segment 1 hiring manager visits richezamor.com, the demo is the proof. Without the demo, the hook is a claim; with the demo, the hook is verified.

## How to apply
- **Pin the demo URL on the LinkedIn profile.** Featured section, position 1.
- **Reference the demo in every Segment 4 outreach.** Speaker pitches, podcast pitches, CFP submissions all link to the live demo.
- **Embed the demo in the Wednesday deep dive cadence.** Periodically (every 6–8 weeks) post a demo walkthrough with a specific framework angle.
- **Use the demo as the X CTA.** Last tweet of an X thread links to the demo.
- **Track demo visits separately in analytics.** Distinguishes "site visit" from "demo engagement"; the latter is a Segment 1/4 conversion proxy.

## Examples
- A ProductTank NYC organizer DMs after a Segment 2 peer mentioned Riché. The DM closes with "the demo at richezamor.com is what made me reach out — that's exactly the kind of practitioner content our audience wants." Demo did the work.
- During the ProductTank NYC talk, Riché demos the Context Layer Engine live on stage. The audience records clips; one clip becomes a Wednesday LinkedIn post; that post pulls 2 inbound podcast pitches.
- A founder DMs Riché after seeing the demo URL in a quote-tweet. The DM: "Can we book 30 min — your retrieval architecture maps to a problem we're stuck on." Demo → advisory inquiry within 48 hours.
