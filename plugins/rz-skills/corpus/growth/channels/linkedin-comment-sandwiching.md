# LinkedIn Comment Sandwiching

## What it is
The pattern of engaging in comments BEFORE and AFTER your own post — both on others' posts and on the comment thread of your own post. The LinkedIn algorithm rewards active users, and comment sandwiching is the practical embodiment of that reward structure.

## Why it matters
LinkedIn's algorithm weights "active engagement" heavily — accounts that comment, reply, and engage outside their own posts get more reach when they do post. Posting and then disappearing for the day signals dormancy and depresses the post's reach. Sandwiching the post inside an active engagement window 30–60 minutes before and 60–90 minutes after maximizes algorithmic distribution.

## How to apply
- **30 minutes before posting:** spend the daily commenting block on target accounts. Substantive comments warm up the algorithm.
- **Post.** Schedule has been the Sunday batch; if posting live, hit "publish" mid-morning.
- **First 60 minutes after posting:** reply to every comment on your own post substantively. Don't drop "thanks!" — engage.
- **2–4 hours after posting:** circle back to other peers' posts and comment again. Sustains the active-engagement signal.
- **Don't fake it.** If a comment thread on your post takes off, prioritize that. Don't paste-bomb other people's posts to game the algorithm.

## Examples
- Wednesday morning, 9:00 AM: Riché spends 15 min commenting on Lenny, Shreyas, Cagan posts. 9:30 AM: posts the Wednesday deep dive on context architecture. 9:45–10:30 AM: replies to first 6 commenters with substantive responses. 1:00 PM: returns to comment on a Teresa Torres post and a peer's post. Algorithm reads "active user"; reach holds.
- A peer's reply to the Wednesday deep dive is a substantive critique. Riché spends 8 minutes drafting a response (not "great point!"). The reply pulls 4 more peers into the thread. Comment thread compounds.
- Bad pattern: post and walk away. Reach plateaus at 2 hours; later peers don't surface.

## Anti-patterns
- Spamming comments to game the algorithm. The substance rule still applies; low-quality comments hurt reputation.
- Posting and disappearing for the day. The first 2 hours after post are the highest-leverage engagement window.
