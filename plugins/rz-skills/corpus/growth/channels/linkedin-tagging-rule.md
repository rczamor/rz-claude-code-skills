# LinkedIn Tagging Rule

## What it is
Tag people in posts and comments only when genuinely referencing their work. Tagging without genuine reference is a transparent attention-grab; it signals desperation, not respect. The rule: if the tag is removable without changing the substance, don't tag.

## Why it matters
The 18 LinkedIn target accounts (Lenny, Shreyas, Cagan, etc.) get hundreds of tag notifications a week — most are spam. Riché's tags only land if they're so substantively grounded that the recipient can't easily ignore them. A tag with a thin reference is filtered as noise; a tag with a genuine extension is read. The discipline of "only tag when genuine" is what makes the rare tag effective.

## How to apply
- **Test:** if you remove the tag, does the post or comment lose meaning? If no, don't tag.
- **Tag with substance:** "Building on @LennyRachitsky's frame about PMF metrics, what I'd add for AI products is..." — the tag is the bridge to a genuine extension.
- **Don't tag in hopes of a like.** Tagging Lenny on a generic AI post hoping he'll engage is the wrong reason.
- **Tag peers when you genuinely build on their work.** When Riché's post is downstream of a Shreyas frame, tag Shreyas. Reciprocity loop opens naturally.
- **One tag per post is usually plenty.** Multi-tag posts read as broadcast spam.

## Examples
- Wrong: "Great thread on PMF this week — what do @LennyRachitsky, @ShreyasDoshi, @MartyCagan think?" Reads as a beg for engagement.
- Right: "@LennyRachitsky's frame on PMF metrics maps to AI products with one wrinkle: you're not measuring product-market fit, you're measuring context fit. Here's what that looks like in practice..." Tag is structural to the post.
- Right (in a comment): on a Cagan post about discovery, Riché replies "The principle holds — what I'd add for AI PM contexts is that the discovery question is upstream of the product. We had to externalize the user's context surface area at Suzy before we could prioritize features." No tag needed; the substance is the point.

## Anti-patterns
- Tagging the entire 18-target-account list in a single post. Spam-flag bait; reputation damage.
- Removing the tag would lose nothing. That's the test for whether the tag is performative.
