# Strategic Commenting (15–20 min/day, LinkedIn)

## What it is
The single highest-ROI activity in the entire growth system. 15–20 minutes per day on LinkedIn, leaving substantive comments on posts by the 18 target accounts (Lenny Rachitsky, Shreyas Doshi, Melissa Perri, Marty Cagan, Aakash Gupta, John Cutler, Gibson Biddle, Petra Wille, Julie Zhuo, Sachin Rekhi, Dan Olsen, Ravi Mehta, Teresa Torres, Marily Nika, Mike Krieger, Akshay Kothari, Kevin Weil, Britt Jamison) and on adjacent peer content.

## Why it matters
Commenting is leverage on someone else's audience. A substantive comment on a Lenny Rachitsky post is read by everyone who reads that post — typically thousands of senior PMs. Compared to posting alone (where reach is bounded by your follower count), commenting routes substance through other people's reach. It's also the dominant mechanism for Segment 2 relationship building: the 18 target accounts notice consistent, substantive commenters over time.

## How to apply
- **Block 15–20 minutes daily.** Most days, less; pick the daily commenting block as the morning ritual.
- **Comment with a perspective, never agreement.** "Great post!" is invisible. See `corpus/growth/playbook/comment-substance-rule.md`.
- **Cycle through the target accounts.** See `corpus/growth/target-accounts/linkedin.yaml` for the personalization angle for each. Goal: 3–5 substantive comments per day across the rotation.
- **Engage the post AND the comment thread.** Replying to a peer's comment under a Shreyas post compounds — both Shreyas and the peer notice.
- **Track results.** Which comments pulled DMs? Which lit up replies from senior PMs? Patterns reveal voice + topic combinations that work.

## Examples
- On Lenny Rachitsky's post about PMF metrics: comment with the context-architecture angle. "PMF for AI products is downstream of context fit, not just product-market fit. Most teams converge on UX before they've stabilized the retrieval surface — and the disengagement that follows reads as PMF failure when it's actually context drift." Two senior PMs reply. One DMs.
- On a Shreyas Doshi post about prioritization: build on his frame with the practitioner angle. "The principle holds; what I'd add for AI PM contexts is that the highest-priority bet is usually invisible: you're prioritizing context surface area before features." Shreyas likes the comment.
- On a Teresa Torres post about discovery: name a tension her post implies. "Discovery for AI products has a wrinkle — the user can't articulate the context they need. The discovery practice has to externalize what's usually implicit."

## Anti-patterns
- Drive-by likes and "great post!" comments. Invisible to the post owner; invisible to other readers.
- Hijacking someone else's post to pitch yourself. Comments build on the host's frame; pitches break it.
