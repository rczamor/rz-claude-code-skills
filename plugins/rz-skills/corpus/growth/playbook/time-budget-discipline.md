# Time Budget Discipline

## What it is
The hard rule: refuse anything that doesn't fit in 5.25 hours/week. Not "try to fit it." Refuse, defer, or scope down. The budget is real, and growth activities have unlimited demand for time — so the only thing protecting the rest of Riché's life (PM work, AI Researcher routine, paid engagements, family) is the discipline of refusing.

## Why it matters
Personal-brand work is the most insidious time-eater because each individual ask seems reasonable: "It's just one podcast pitch," "It's just one extra post," "It's just one more comment." Each one is small; together they balloon to 10–15 hours per week and crowd out the work that actually pays. The discipline is what makes the system sustainable for 12+ months instead of burning out by month 4.

## How to apply
- **Refusal vocabulary:** practice saying no in concrete forms. "I can't fit that this quarter." "That doesn't fit my current cadence." "Defer to Q3." Don't apologize for the budget.
- **Defer to quarterly review.** When new opportunities (newsletter, second podcast, Substack) arise, defer them to the next quarterly review (`corpus/growth/metrics/quarterly-review-checklist.md`). If they still belong, swap something out.
- **Track overruns.** The dashboard logs weekly hours. Two consecutive overrun weeks trigger a re-balance — drop the lowest-leverage activity.
- **Honor the budget under social pressure.** Peer asks ("can you co-author?"), opportunity FOMO ("everyone's on Threads"), and platform shifts will pull you off-budget. Hold the line.

## Examples
- A peer asks Riché to co-author a deep dive that would take 4 hours. The full week's writing budget is 2 hours. Riché replies: "I can't co-author this quarter — happy to swap one short reaction post for a co-authored thread, but a deep-dive collab doesn't fit." Honest, specific, no apology.
- A Substack alternative gets buzz. Rather than adding it, Riché defers to the next quarterly review. At review, asks: would it replace LinkedIn or X presence? No. Skip.
- Newsletter is "deferred to Q3 2026 pending ~1,500–2,000 LinkedIn followers." That deferral is the discipline in action — the activity is real but doesn't fit yet.

## Anti-patterns
- "Just this once" exceptions that compound. One exception per month is one exception too many.
- Adding new channels without removing existing ones. Each addition needs an explicit subtraction.
