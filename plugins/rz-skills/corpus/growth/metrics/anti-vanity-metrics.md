# Anti-Vanity Metrics Rule

## What it is
Followers and impressions are vanity metrics unless tied to segment proxies. The rule: don't track followers or impressions as primary metrics. Track them as context for segment proxies. A 10K-follower account whose audience is junior PMs and bots is less valuable than a 2K-follower account whose audience is senior PMs at AI companies.

## Why it matters
Vanity metrics drive the wrong behavior. Optimizing for follower count produces engagement-bait posts that inflate count and degrade Segment 2 trust. Optimizing for impressions produces clickbait headlines that lose Segment 1 credibility. The vanity metrics aren't *wrong* — they exist for a reason — but they become wrong when they replace segment proxies as the steering signal.

## How to apply
- **Don't post follower count screenshots.** Even when growth is real, posting "just hit 5K!" reads as performative and signals vanity-metric optimization.
- **Don't track impressions as a primary metric.** They're context — use them only when paired with a segment proxy ("18% of impressions came from senior PMs at AI companies").
- **Refuse content tactics that exist only to grow numbers.** Engagement-bait, viral hooks, "rage bait" — short-term numbers that destroy long-term credibility.
- **When asked "how many followers do you have?" answer with the underlying signal.** "I'm not optimizing for follower count — what I track is Segment 2 reshares and peer DMs, and those are up 40% this quarter."

## Examples
- A peer asks "how do I get to 10K followers?" Riché reframes: "What's your Segment 2 reshare count from 1K+ accounts? That's the lever, not follower count."
- LinkedIn shows a viral post pulled 50K impressions but only 2 substantive senior-PM comments. The post is logged as "high vanity, low quality" — pattern to avoid.
- A Wednesday deep dive on context architecture pulls 3K impressions but 4 reshares from 2K+ follower senior PMs. Logged as "low vanity, high quality" — pattern to repeat.

## Anti-patterns
- Adopting "viral content" tactics. Viral content optimizes for vanity metrics and degrades Segment 2 trust.
- Letting follower count anxiety drive the Sunday writing session. The session is for substance, not for numbers.
