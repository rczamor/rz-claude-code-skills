# Quarterly Review Checklist

## What it is
The end-of-quarter audit. A 60–90 minute session that reviews the prior 13 weekly dashboards, identifies patterns, and decides what to keep, change, or drop for the next quarter. Run on the last day of the quarter or the first day of the next.

## Why it matters
Weekly dashboards are tactical (steer mid-quarter). Quarterly reviews are strategic (decide what gets a whole new quarter and what gets dropped). Without quarterly review, the system runs on inertia — last quarter's tactics keep running even if they've stopped working. The review is the swap mechanism that keeps the time budget honest and the strategy adaptive.

## How to apply

**Section 1: Lagging indicators (15 min)**
- Recruiter DMs received this quarter
- Speaking invitations accepted
- Advisory inquiries / engagements
- Job interviews / offers
- Follower count growth (context only, not steering)

**Section 2: Segment performance (15 min)**
- Which segments grew? Which stagnated?
- Best-performing posts per segment (top 2 per quarter)
- Which channels paid off? Which to drop?

**Section 3: Time budget audit (10 min)**
- Average weekly hours per playbook activity
- Overruns and their causes
- What to drop? What to add?

**Section 4: Hook and positioning audit (10 min)**
- Did the hook keep landing? Any audience confusion?
- Are credentials still credible (Helm Labs metrics still relevant; Suzy still recent)?
- Anything to refresh on the LinkedIn profile, speaker one-sheet, website?

**Section 5: Next quarter plan (15 min)**
- 3 focus segments and what to do for each
- 2–3 events / podcasts to pitch
- 1 thing to drop, 1 thing to test

**Section 6: Lessons (10 min)**
- 2–3 sentences on what surprised you this quarter
- Patterns to watch next quarter

## Examples
- Q1 2026 review:
  - Lagging: 4 inbound recruiter DMs (up 100% vs Q4), 1 speaking invitation accepted (ProductTank NYC), 1 advisory inquiry. Job offers: 0 (lagging by 4–8 weeks per pattern, expected in Q2).
  - Segments: S1 modest growth, S2 strong (reshares up 40%), S3 emerging (4 founder DMs), S4 first wins (1 invitation, 1 CFP accepted).
  - Time: averaged 5.4 hrs/week (slight overrun, caused by ProductTank prep — acceptable). 
  - Hook: holding well; consider refreshing the LinkedIn About after the talk.
  - Next quarter: focus S2 + S4. Pitch Mind the Product CFP (Oct 6-7). Drop X-engagement target accounts that produced no signal in Q1. Test a Wednesday article cadence (vs. post).
- A drop decision: X engagement with @gibsonbiddle produced 1 reply across 12 weeks. Drop from active rotation; rotate in a different account.

## Anti-patterns
- Skipping quarterly review. The system runs on inertia and stops adapting.
- Reviewing without making swap decisions. The point is to drop what isn't working, not just to log it.
