# Tiered Packaging

## What it is
A pricing structure with multiple plans (typically Starter, Pro, Enterprise — or Free, Plus, Premium, Business) where each tier unlocks additional features, capacity, support, or capabilities. The mechanism captures different willingness-to-pay across customer segments without forcing a single price point. Three-tier packaging is most common because behavioral economics shows three options optimize the conversion path: a cheap option to anchor low, a premium to anchor high, and a middle that most customers select (the "decoy effect").

## Why it matters
A single price point under-monetizes the high-WTP segment and prices out the low-WTP segment. Tiered packaging captures both. The middle tier typically generates the most revenue (the "Goldilocks" effect), the top tier captures the high-WTP enterprise, and the bottom tier or freemium tier handles top-of-funnel acquisition. Done well, tiered packaging is the single highest-leverage monetization mechanism in SaaS.

## How to apply
1. **Use three or four tiers, not five+.** Cognitive load rises sharply past four; conversion drops.
2. **Differentiate tiers by value driver, not feature count.** Each tier should serve a clear segment with a clear job.
3. **Anchor the high tier.** Make Enterprise visibly more expensive (often "Contact us") to anchor the middle tier as reasonable.
4. **Watch the upgrade triggers.** What forces a customer from one tier to the next? Capacity? New feature? Team size? Support tier?
5. **Pricing tier tests.** Van Westendorp + conjoint can validate tier pricing pre-launch. Post-launch, monitor upgrade rates between tiers and optimize.

## Examples
1. **HubSpot's three-tier packaging** (Starter / Professional / Enterprise) anchored against company growth stage. Each tier targets a distinct segment with a distinct job.
2. **Notion's tiers** (Free / Plus / Business / Enterprise) — clean upgrade triggers (collaboration → admin → security/SSO).
3. **Suzy's enterprise tier model** for 350+ enterprise customers likely uses tier differentiation across the Intelligence/Insights/Impact pillars and seat/usage capacity.

## Anti-patterns
1. **Feature-count differentiation.** "Tier B has 2x the features of Tier A." Customers see this as price discrimination, not value tiering. Differentiate by value driver.
2. **Five-plus tiers.** Cognitive load spikes; conversion rates drop. Most products underuse three-tier structures and overuse complex matrices.
