# DHM Margin Lens

## What it is
The third leg of Gibson Biddle's DHM Model — the **business-model and margin** question. From the product-management skill verbatim: "Product strategy answers: 'How will your product delight customers in hard-to-copy, margin-enhancing ways?' Three questions: what delights? what's hard to copy? what experiments with business model?" The margin lens forces every product decision to engage with monetization economics — pricing model, packaging, gross margin, and contribution per customer — not just feature delight or competitive defensibility.

## Why it matters
Product teams default to delight-and-defensibility thinking and treat monetization as Finance's problem. Biddle's third leg forces the product team to own the business-model question. Without it, products win delight, win defensibility, and lose money on every customer — see the venture-capital graveyard of beautifully designed PLG products with broken unit economics. The margin lens is what separates strategy from product.

## How to apply
1. **Ask the third question on every strategic decision.** Does this delight? Is it hard to copy? Does it experiment with the business model in a margin-enhancing direction?
2. **Tie product decisions to gross margin.** Adding a feature that requires expensive infrastructure (proprietary models, manual ops) without raising willingness-to-pay degrades margin.
3. **Experiment with pricing as a product.** Move from seat-based to usage-based, or add a free tier, as deliberately as you'd ship a new feature. Both require hypothesis, experiment, data, conclusion.
4. **Watch the cost-to-serve.** AI products especially: token costs scale with usage. The margin equation can flip overnight if you don't model it.
5. **Find margin-enhancing experiments.** Subscription tiers, expansion packaging, usage caps, free → paid conversion mechanisms.

## Examples
1. **Netflix's pivot from DVD-by-mail to streaming** under Reed Hastings was a margin-enhancing business-model experiment — gross margin restructured from physical-media costs to content-licensing economics.
2. **Snowflake's usage-based pricing** is the classic margin-enhancing model — customer success directly correlates with revenue growth, NRR > 160% at scale.
3. **Riché's Suzy with 350+ enterprise customers** — DHM margin lens applies to every Decision Engine feature decision: does this feature justify pricing power? Does it enable expansion or just retention?

## Anti-patterns
1. **Margin-blind delight.** Building beloved features that destroy gross margin (manual support, expensive AI calls, custom work). Beautiful product, broken business.
2. **Treating monetization as Finance's job.** The product team that doesn't engage with pricing/packaging cedes control of the customer relationship.
