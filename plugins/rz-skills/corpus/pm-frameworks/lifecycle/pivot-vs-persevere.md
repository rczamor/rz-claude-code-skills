# Pivot vs. Persevere

## What it is
A binary decision in Eric Ries's Lean Startup loop. After each Build-Measure-Learn cycle, the team must decide: **persevere** (the hypothesis is being validated, keep iterating in the same direction) or **pivot** (the hypothesis is falsified, change strategy). Ries lists ten pivot types: zoom-in (one feature becomes the product), zoom-out (the product becomes one feature), customer segment, customer need, platform, business architecture, value capture, engine of growth, channel, technology.

## Why it matters
From Ries, *The Lean Startup* (2011). Without the explicit decision, teams default to perseverance through inertia — sunk cost, narrative coherence, and the difficulty of admitting the original thesis was wrong. The forcing function of pivot vs. persevere forces evidence to surface: what would falsify our current direction? If nothing would, we are not learning.

## How to apply
1. **Run the meeting on a cadence.** Most lean teams hold a pivot/persevere meeting every 4–8 weeks, post-cohort.
2. **Bring the data.** Cohort retention curves, conversion rates, qualitative interview themes. Vanity metrics (signups) cannot trigger a pivot decision.
3. **Anchor on the leap-of-faith hypotheses.** Has value-hypothesis evidence improved or worsened? Has growth-hypothesis evidence improved or worsened?
4. **Name the pivot type.** "We're pivoting" is too vague. "We're doing a customer-segment pivot from SMB to mid-market" is actionable.
5. **Persevere ≠ status quo.** Persevering still means iterating on tactics. The decision is direction, not pace.

## Examples
1. **Slack** — Tiny Speck's original product was the game *Glitch*. The pivot was a *zoom-in* (the internal chat tool became the product) plus a *customer-segment* pivot (gamers → distributed teams).
2. **Instagram** — pivoted from Burbn (a Foursquare-like check-in app with photos) to photo-sharing only. Classic zoom-in pivot.
3. **Riché's Grandstage iteration** — narrowed from broad GTM to a single buyer profile after early evidence; not a full pivot, but a customer-segment refinement that produced 300% growth at $0 CAC.

## Anti-patterns
1. **Pivot fatigue.** Calling every minor adjustment a pivot. Word loses meaning, team loses confidence.
2. **Persevere by default.** Skipping the explicit decision. Sunk cost wins quietly. Cure: schedule the meeting on a calendar and require it.
