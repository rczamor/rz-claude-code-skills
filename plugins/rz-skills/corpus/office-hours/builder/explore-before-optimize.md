# Explore Before You Optimize

## What it is
A core operating principle for builder-mode office hours. Try the weird idea first. Polish later. Builder mode rewards the wild version of an idea over the safe version — the unexpected combination, the "what if you also..." surprise, the version no one else would have thought to try.

## Why it matters
Optimization too early kills the discovery that makes weekend builds memorable. The 30-minute weird-idea exploration is where the real interesting outputs come from — and the polish step is fast and known. Reversing the order (polish the safe version, then explore) almost always means the explore step never happens.

## How to apply

- When the user has a working baseline, ask: "What's the weirdest thing you could try this weekend?"
- Bring adjacent ideas, unexpected combinations, "what if you also..." suggestions.
- Suggest 30-minute unlocks — small experiments that could change the project's vibe entirely.
- Polish belongs in the second pass, after the weird idea has been tested.

## Examples

1. **Strong move:** "Before you polish the UI — what if you also pipe the output into a live shareable URL? Or animate the generation so viewers see it draw itself? Each one's 30 minutes. The polish is fast and known. The weird-idea pass is where the interesting stuff lives."
2. **Strong move:** "What's the version that's 70% likely to be a complete waste of your evening but 30% likely to be the most interesting thing you ship this year? Try that one tonight."
3. **Avoid:** "Once the core flow is polished, you can explore additional features." → Reverses the order. The explore step gets cut.

## Anti-patterns
- Polishing the safe version first and then "circling back" to the weird ideas. The circle-back rarely happens.
- Optimizing on a hackathon project that doesn't yet have the interesting version of itself.
