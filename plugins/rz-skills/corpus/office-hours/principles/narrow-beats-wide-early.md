# Narrow Beats Wide, Early

## What it is
A non-negotiable operating principle in startup-mode office hours. The smallest version someone will pay real money for this week is more valuable than the full platform vision. Wedge first. Expand from strength.

## Why it matters
Platforms ship in months and reveal nothing until shipped. Wedges ship in days and reveal whether anyone wants any of this. Early-stage founders who can't articulate a paid wedge are usually attached to architecture rather than value. Once you have a wedge that works, you've earned the right to expand. Until then, the platform vision is a hypothesis dressed up as a product.

## How to apply

- Ask: "What's the smallest version someone would pay for this week?" If the answer involves "but we'd need to build the rest first," push.
- The wedge should be describable in one sentence and shippable in days, not months.
- Bonus: "What if the user didn't have to do anything at all to get value? No login, no integration, no setup. What would that look like?" That's often the real wedge.
- Resistance like "we could strip it down but then it wouldn't be differentiated" usually means the differentiation isn't in the platform — it's in one specific insight or workflow.

## Examples

1. **Platform attachment:** "We're building an end-to-end revenue platform." **Push:** "What's the one thing a user would pay $200/mo for this week, before any of the rest exists?"
2. **Real wedge:** "A weekly email that flags the 5 deals at risk this week, no integration needed, $200/month. Ships in 3 days." → Specific, monetized, no setup tax.
3. **Zero-setup wedge:** "A public URL where you paste your CRM export and get back a deal-risk score in 30 seconds. Free for the first 100 deals, $200/mo above that." → Even sharper.

## Anti-patterns
- Defining the wedge as "the MVP version of the platform." A wedge is a standalone thing someone pays for.
- Insisting the platform is necessary for differentiation. Usually the differentiation is one specific insight buried inside the platform — extract it.
- Treating the bonus push (zero-setup version) as a thought experiment. It's often the actual wedge.
