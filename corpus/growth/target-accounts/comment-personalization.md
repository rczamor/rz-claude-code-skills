# Comment Personalization Rule

## What it is
Each target account gets a personalized comment angle that reflects their public work and Riché's positioning. Generic templated comments fail across all 18 LinkedIn targets and 9 X targets. The rule: every comment is shaped by what THIS specific thinker writes about, not a copy-paste of "great post about AI PM!"

## Why it matters
Personalization is what makes the 15–20 min/day commenting block compound into relationships. A Cagan-personalized comment on a Cagan post lands; a generic comment on the same post is filtered. Over weeks and months, the personalization is what makes the 18 target accounts notice Riché's name and read his comments first when they appear.

## How to apply
- **Per account, hold a stable comment angle that maps Riché's positioning to their public work.** See `corpus/growth/target-accounts/linkedin.yaml` for the canonical angle per account.
- **Each comment is shaped by the specific post, not just the angle.** Read the post fully; identify what's not addressed; build on or refine.
- **Reference their prior work when it's genuine.** "Building on your earlier piece about X..." — but only if you've actually read X.
- **Show familiarity without flattery.** Knowing the thinker's catalog signals respect. Performing knowledge of it signals try-hard.

## Examples
- **Lenny Rachitsky angle:** context architecture as missing layer in AI PM. On a Lenny PMF post: "PMF for AI products has a hidden floor — context fit. Most converge on UX before stabilizing retrieval; early disengagement reads as PMF failure when it's context drift."
- **Shreyas Doshi angle:** AI PM-specific extensions to his frameworks. On a Shreyas prioritization post: "The principle holds; what I'd add for AI PMs is that the highest-priority bet is usually invisible — context surface area before features."
- **Cagan angle:** discovery for AI products with context constraints. On a Cagan discovery post: "Discovery for AI products has a wrinkle — the user can't articulate the context they need. The discovery practice has to externalize what's usually implicit."
- **Teresa Torres angle:** discovery when users can't articulate context. On an OST post: "OSTs work for AI products until you hit the context layer — the user's opportunities aren't always knowable to them."

## Anti-patterns
- Generic comments that could appear on any post. The audience and the host both filter.
- Performative familiarity ("loved your 2019 piece on...") without actually engaging with what's there.
