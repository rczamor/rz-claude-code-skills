# Comment → DM Escalation Cadence

## What it is
The cadence for moving a target-account relationship from public comments to direct messages to real-world meetings. The pattern: weekly substantive comment → DM after 3–4 substantive exchanges → real-world meet at a conference or coffee. Premature escalation kills the relationship; over-cautious escalation lets it stagnate.

## Why it matters
Most personal-brand operators get this cadence wrong in one of two ways: (1) escalate too fast (DM after first comment exchange — reads as transactional), or (2) never escalate (stay in comment threads forever — relationship plateaus). The 3–4 substantive exchange threshold plus the real-world meet at events is the empirical pattern that converts to lasting peer relationships.

## How to apply
- **Stage 1: weekly substantive comments.** On the target's posts, in the 15–20 min daily block. Personalize per `corpus/growth/target-accounts/comment-personalization.md`. No DM yet.
- **Stage 2: target acknowledges or replies.** Watch for the target liking your comment, replying to it, or referencing it elsewhere. This is the readiness signal.
- **Stage 3: 3–4 substantive exchanges.** A comment + reply + follow-up + their next post you engaged on. Genuine back-and-forth.
- **Stage 4: DM with a specific value.** "Your post on X mapped to a problem we hit at Suzy. Wrote up the diagnostic — happy to send if useful." Specific, valuable, no pitch.
- **Stage 5: real-world meet.** ProductTank NYC, Mind the Product, SF/NY trips. Coffee or 15-min introduction.

The full timeline: 4–8 weeks from first comment to DM, 2–6 months from DM to in-person.

## Examples
- Riché comments substantively on Lenny Rachitsky posts for 6 weeks. Lenny replies to one comment ("good frame"). After 3 more exchanges, Riché DMs with a specific resource. Lenny's team responds with a podcast booking inquiry within a week. The cadence worked.
- A senior PM target gets weekly comments from Riché. After 4 exchanges, the PM replies "we should connect." Riché DMs the next day with a specific reason and a 15-min coffee suggestion. Connection deepens.
- Wrong: cold DM Lenny after first comment. Reads as candidate; pattern-matches to dozens of weekly cold pitches. Killed before it started.
- Wrong: 6 months of comments with no DM. The relationship plateaus at "name recognition" but never becomes peer-trust.
