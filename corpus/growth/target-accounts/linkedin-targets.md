# LinkedIn Target Accounts (18)

## What it is
The 18 LinkedIn accounts to comment on regularly. Roster: Lenny Rachitsky, Shreyas Doshi, Melissa Perri, Marty Cagan, Aakash Gupta, John Cutler, Gibson Biddle, Petra Wille, Julie Zhuo, Sachin Rekhi, Dan Olsen, Ravi Mehta, Teresa Torres, Marily Nika, Mike Krieger, Akshay Kothari, Kevin Weil, Britt Jamison.

The structured per-account data (focus area, comment angle for Riché's positioning) lives in `corpus/growth/target-accounts/linkedin.yaml`. The PM-thinker entries (deeper background on each) live in `corpus/pm-frameworks/thinkers/`.

## Why it matters
Without a target list, the 15–20 min/day commenting block dissipates. The 18-account list is bounded enough to rotate through weekly and broad enough to reach Segment 2 from multiple angles (Lenny's audience overlaps Cagan's overlaps Torres's, but each angle adds something distinct).

## How to apply
- **Daily rotation in the 15–20 min commenting block.** Aim for 3–5 substantive comments daily, rotating across the 18 accounts over a week.
- **Personalize the angle to each.** Each target has a specific comment angle that reflects Riché's positioning + their public work — see `corpus/growth/target-accounts/linkedin.yaml`.
- **Track which accounts notice.** Some thinkers actively engage with substantive commenters (Lenny, Shreyas, Cagan); others rarely do (Mike Krieger, Kevin Weil are more selective). Adjust expectations.
- **Don't skip the smaller accounts.** Britt Jamison or Marily Nika may have smaller audiences but stronger engagement; reciprocity loops are easier to open.

## Examples
- On a Lenny Rachitsky post about PMF metrics: Riché comments with the context-architecture angle ("PMF for AI products is downstream of context fit, not just product-market fit"). Two senior PMs reply.
