# The Flywheel Starts With Relationships, Not Content

## What it is
The single most important rule for the entire growth strategy: the flywheel is anchored on Segment 2 relationships, not content production. Without peers who reshare, comment, and refer, content stays in a small echo chamber regardless of how good it is.

## Why it matters
Most personal-brand strategies inverted: produce more content, hope for reach. That model is downstream of the algorithm and brittle to platform changes. The relationship-first model is robust: even if LinkedIn's algorithm shifts, peers who know and trust Riché's work continue to reshare, refer, and recommend. The compounding asset is the network, not the content library.

## How to apply
- Spend the first quarter (or first month if starting from a base) on Segment 2 relationships before optimizing content. See `corpus/networking/` for relationship-building tactics.
- Allocate the largest portion of the weekly time budget to *engaging on others' content* (15–20 min/day commenting), not just producing your own. See `corpus/growth/playbook/strategic-commenting.md`.
- When metrics are weak, ask "where's the relationship gap?" before "where's the content gap?"
- Treat ProductTank, conferences, and Slack communities as relationship infrastructure, not content distribution.
- Reference the canonical analog from Andrew Chen's *Cold Start Problem*: seed the network before scaling the content (see `corpus/growth/metrics/cold-start-personal-brand.md`).

## Examples
- A new senior PM Riché met at ProductTank NYC reshares his Wednesday deep dive on context architecture two weeks later. That reshare lands the post on a VP's feed at a Forbes AI 50 company. The relationship preceded the reach.
- During the Suzy launch, peers who Riché had been engaging with for a quarter amplified the launch announcement. Without those existing relationships, the launch post would have stayed inside Riché's direct followers — maybe 200 views. With relationships, it reached an order of magnitude more.
- A peer from Reforge Slack offers an intro to Lenny Rachitsky's podcast booker. Six months of substantive Slack engagement made the intro natural; cold-pitching the booker would have been ignored.

## Anti-patterns
- Doubling content output when reshares are flat. The bottleneck is relationships, not content volume.
- Treating networking as separate from growth. They're the same flywheel; networking is what makes growth compound.
