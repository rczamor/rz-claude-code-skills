# Don't Post Without an Audience Segment Target

## What it is
The rule: every post is written for a specific audience segment, with that segment's filter applied during the writing session. Posts written "for everyone" land for nobody. The discipline: at the top of each Sunday-batch draft, name the target segment (S1, S2, S3, S4) and the proxy this post is designed to move.

## Why it matters
Generic posts are the second most common failure mode (after echo chamber). They feel safe — they offend no one and please no one. But Segment 2 reads them as generic-PM-content, Segment 3 reads them as PM-not-founder content, and Segment 1 doesn't see them at all because Segment 2 didn't reshare. Targeting forces sharpness; sharpness produces reshares.

## How to apply
- **At the top of each draft, name the segment.** "This post is for S2 — Wednesday deep dive on context architecture aimed at senior PMs at AI companies. Proxy: reshare from 1K+ accounts."
- **Apply the segment's voice and substance filter.** S2 wants peer voice with sharp positions. S3 wants technical depth and shipping experience. S1 doesn't read posts; S2 reaches them.
- **If you can't name a segment for the post, don't post it.** Either sharpen the angle or replace the post with a different topic.
- **A single post can have a primary and secondary segment.** "Primary S2 (peer reshare); secondary S4 (event organizers reading via S2 reshare)." But it must have a clear primary.

## Examples
- Wrong: "AI is transforming product management" — generic; no segment; lands nowhere.
- Right: Wednesday deep dive aimed at S2: "Most AI PM job descriptions miss the context layer entirely. They list 'prompt engineering' or 'model selection' — the surface, not the substrate. The job actually requires context architecture: how the system represents what the user knows, has done, and intends." Sharp; targeted; reshareable.
- Right: Tuesday signal aimed at S3: a quote-tweet of a Sachin Rekhi take with the founder-specific extension. "What's missing for AI founders building product from scratch is that retrieval architecture is the first roadmap decision, not the third."

## Anti-patterns
- "It's a general PM thought" — generic posts that try to please all segments and land for none.
- Posting to fill the cadence rather than because the segment-targeted post is sharp. Better to post 3x/week sharp than 5x/week generic.
