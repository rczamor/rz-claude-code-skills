# Don't Try to Reach Segment 1 Directly

## What it is
The rule: never reach Segment 1 (AI Product Hiring Managers — VP/CPO-level) directly. They are reached only through Segments 2 (peer reshares, peer referrals) and 3 (founder advisory, where founders refer up to investors and VPs). Direct outreach (cold DMs, direct content pitches, "I'd be a great fit at your company" notes) is the dominant failure mode.

## Why it matters
Segment 1 is the conversion target for Riché's career — and conversion targets are exactly where credibility is most fragile. A direct DM from a stranger reads as candidate noise. A peer reshare with a substantive comment reads as social proof. The first costs reputation; the second buys it. Treating Segment 1 as a content-consumption audience inverts the funnel and produces the wrong kind of attention.

## How to apply
- **Filter every Segment 1 outreach instinct through this question:** "Is there a Segment 2 or Segment 3 person who can introduce me?" If yes, ask for the intro. If no, build that network first.
- **When a Segment 1 leader engages** (profile view, like, connection request), treat as signal — but don't escalate the relationship until they DM first or a mutual broker exists.
- **Optimize the LinkedIn profile and pinned posts** for the *passive* Segment 1 reader who lands there via peer reshare. The hook does the work — see `corpus/growth/channels/linkedin-profile-optimization.md`.
- **Reserve direct outreach for Segments 2, 3, and 4.** Segment 1 outreach is always indirect.

## Examples
- A VP of Product at an AI startup likes Riché's post. Wrong move: DM her with "thanks for the like, here's my deck." Right move: note the engagement, continue posting consistently, wait for her to move first or for a peer to broker.
- A CPO at a Forbes AI 50 company is hiring. Wrong move: cold pitch. Right move: identify Segment 2 peers in that CPO's network, build relationships there, let referral pull do the work.
- "Just a quick note to introduce myself" — this is the failure mode. Quick notes from strangers don't convert hiring decisions.

## Anti-patterns
- Treating LinkedIn profile views as invitations to DM. A view is signal to keep posting, not permission to escalate.
- "Just one cold DM, what's the harm?" The harm is reputational and compounds — Segment 1 talks; bad pitches get remembered.
