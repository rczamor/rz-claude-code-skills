# Don't Exceed the 5.25-Hour Time Budget

## What it is
The rule: never let growth activities exceed 5.25 hours per week. The activities have unlimited demand for time; the budget is the only thing keeping the rest of Riché's life (PM work, AI Researcher routine, paid engagements, family) intact. Anything beyond 5.25 hours gets refused, deferred, or scoped down.

## Why it matters
Time-budget overrun is the most insidious failure mode because each individual ask seems reasonable — "It's just one extra post," "It's just one more comment," "It's just a co-author thread." Each is small; together they balloon to 10–15 hours/week and crowd out the actual work that pays. Honoring the budget is what makes the system sustainable for 12+ months instead of burning out by month 4.

## How to apply
- **Track weekly hours in the dashboard** (`corpus/growth/metrics/dashboard-template.md`). Two consecutive overrun weeks trigger a re-balance.
- **Refuse activities that don't fit.** Practice the language: "I can't fit that this quarter." "Defer to Q3." Don't apologize.
- **When an opportunity arrives mid-week** (podcast pitch, big content opportunity), triage: is this Tier 1? If yes, swap something out *this week*. If no, defer.
- **Remove before adding.** Newsletter without dropping something else = budget overrun. Pick what gets dropped first.
- **Honor under social pressure.** Peer asks ("can you co-author?"), opportunity FOMO ("everyone's doing this new platform"), and platform shifts will pull off-budget. Hold the line.

## Examples
- Sunday writing session ran 3.5 hours instead of 2. Cause: Wednesday deep dive grew. Fix: narrow next Wednesday's scope. The constraint forces sharper writing.
- A peer asks Riché to co-author a 4-hour piece. Full week's writing budget is 2 hours. Riché replies: "I can't co-author this quarter — happy to swap one short reaction post for a co-authored thread, but a deep-dive collab doesn't fit." Specific, no apology.
- A Substack alternative gets buzz. Rather than adding, Riché defers to next quarterly review. At review: would it replace LinkedIn or X presence? No. Skip.
- Three weeks of overrun (5.5, 6, 5.7 hours). Diagnosed: ProductTank prep was uncounted. Re-baseline; absorbed prep into the 5.25; discipline restored.

## Anti-patterns
- "Just this week" exceptions that compound. One per month is one too many.
- Adding new channels/activities without explicit subtractions. Each addition needs a deletion.
