# Don't Create Content for Medium

## What it is
The rule: don't publish original content on Medium. Medium's organic distribution has degraded since the 2017 paywall pivot, the SEO equity is split between Medium and the author, and the audience that exists there isn't concentrated in Riché's segments (PMs are on LinkedIn, founders are on X).

## Why it matters
Medium feels like a "thoughtful long-form" channel and pulls writers in. But the activity-to-outcome ratio is poor: hours invested produce neither algorithmic reach (Medium's distribution depends on paid memberships and curated feeds, both shrinking) nor SEO equity (Medium owns the canonical URL). The same content time invested in LinkedIn articles or richezamor.com produces 5–10x the leverage.

## How to apply
- **Don't publish on Medium.** Not original posts, not cross-posts of LinkedIn content.
- **Use LinkedIn articles for long-form** — see `corpus/growth/channels/linkedin-articles.md`. LinkedIn's domain authority and feed integration produce real reach.
- **Use richezamor.com for permanent canonical content** — see `corpus/growth/channels/website-seo-targets.md`. Owned SEO equity.
- **Refuse Medium cross-post requests** when peers ask. Polite "I'm focused on LinkedIn and richezamor.com this year." 
- **If a Medium publication invites a syndication of an existing LinkedIn article**, consider with caution — only if (a) the publication has demonstrable PM/AI-PM audience, (b) the canonical URL stays on richezamor.com or LinkedIn, and (c) it doesn't fragment SEO.

## Examples
- A peer suggests Riché should be on Medium "for the long-form audience." Riché declines: "Medium's distribution has degraded; the same time invested on LinkedIn articles produces measurably more reach."
- A Medium publication editor reaches out asking for original content. Riché replies politely: "I'm focused on LinkedIn and richezamor.com this year. Happy to send a syndication of an existing piece if you keep the canonical URL on the source."
- An old Medium account from years ago has 12 abandoned drafts. Riché archives the account; doesn't re-engage.

## Anti-patterns
- Cross-posting to Medium "for completeness." Splits SEO and confuses canonical URL.
- Reviving old Medium accounts. The activity-to-outcome ratio hasn't changed.
