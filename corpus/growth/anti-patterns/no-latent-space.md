# Don't Engage in Latent Space Community

## What it is
The rule: don't invest time in the Latent Space community (Discord, podcast, conference). Latent Space is engineering-focused, not PM-focused. The audience overlaps lightly with Segment 3 (AI founders) but predominantly with ML engineers and AI infrastructure builders — not Riché's primary segments.

## Why it matters
Latent Space is high-quality and tempts an AI PM to engage there. But the audience density of senior PMs (Segment 2) and product hiring managers (Segment 1) is near-zero. Time invested produces conversations with engineers and infrastructure folks — interesting, but not the right segment for the career conversion target. Honoring the time budget means refusing Latent Space even when the content is genuinely good.

## How to apply
- **Don't post in Latent Space Discord or community channels.** Lurking to learn is fine; investing engagement time isn't.
- **Don't pitch the Latent Space podcast** unless the episode topic is *specifically* a context-architecture / AI product crossover that maps to PMs.
- **Don't speak at Latent Space events** unless the event is explicitly cross-functional (PM + eng + research).
- **Use the time on Segment 2/3 channels instead** — LinkedIn, X target accounts, ProductTank, Reforge Slacks.
- **Re-evaluate quarterly.** If Latent Space's audience shifts toward AI PMs (it might, as the field matures), the rule could change. Currently, no signal.

## Examples
- A founder Riché advises is active in Latent Space and suggests cross-posting there. Riché declines politely: "Latent Space's audience is mostly engineering — my work is for AI PMs. The cross-post wouldn't land."
- A Latent Space podcast episode covers RAG architecture. Riché listens (learning), but doesn't engage in the community channels around it.
- Quarterly check: density of Segment 2/Segment 1 in Latent Space? Still near-zero. Rule holds.

## Anti-patterns
- Engaging because "the content is good." Quality is necessary but not sufficient; the audience must match.
- Treating engineering communities as a back door to AI product roles. The path is through PM peers and product leadership, not through ML engineers.
