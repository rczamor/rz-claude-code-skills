# CFP Template Structure

## What it is
The structure for Call-for-Proposal (CFP) submissions to major conferences (Mind the Product, AI Product Summit, Product-Led Summit, CPO Summit). Five sections: title, abstract, audience takeaways, bio, why-now.

## Why it matters
CFPs are read by committees in batches of 50–500 submissions. The committee scans each in under a minute. A clear structure with sharp content survives the scan; a meandering or generic submission is filtered. The template below is the one that survives.

## How to apply

**1. Title (1 line, 60–90 characters).**
The hook compressed to title length. Not a question; not generic.
Example: "Context Architecture: Why Most AI Products Are Optimizing the Wrong Layer"

**2. Abstract (150–250 words).**
Open with the speaker hook (`corpus/growth/speaking/the-hook.md`). Follow with the position (3–4 sentences on why context architecture is the moat). Close with what the audience walks away with (the live demo is the centerpiece).

**3. Audience takeaways (3–5 bullets).**
Concrete, action-oriented. Not "you'll learn about." Yes "you'll leave with a 5-step diagnostic for context architecture in your own AI product."
- Bullet 1: a framework or diagnostic the audience can apply Monday.
- Bullet 2: a specific pattern from a real product (Suzy or Helm Labs).
- Bullet 3: the live-demo walkthrough.
- Bullet 4 (optional): a counter-position to a common AI PM mistake.
- Bullet 5 (optional): a closing principle that compresses the talk.

**4. Bio (100–150 words).**
Lead with current role and the practitioner credentials. Helm Labs $3.25M pipeline; Suzy 350+ enterprise customers; IBM background. Close with the unique angle (built personal knowledge engine to prove context architecture).

**5. Why-now (50–100 words).**
Why this talk, why this audience, why this conference, why this year. Specific to the venue. Not "AI is hot."

## Examples
- Mind the Product CFP submission for Oct 6-7: title "Context Architecture: Why Most AI Products Are Optimizing the Wrong Layer." Abstract opens with hook. Takeaways include 5-step diagnostic + live demo. Bio leads with Helm Labs metrics. Why-now: "Mind the Product's audience is exactly the senior PM peer group wrestling with AI's hidden architecture decisions. The live demo of the Context Layer Engine has never been shown publicly."
- AI Product Summit CFP: title leads with the live-demo angle for an AI-PM-specific audience. Abstract emphasizes practitioner credentials + technical depth. Takeaways are technical (retrieval architecture, eval setup).
- CPO Summit CFP: title compresses for a leadership audience. Abstract emphasizes strategic implications. Takeaways are leadership-level (org design, hiring patterns, what to fund).

## Anti-patterns
- Generic titles ("Lessons from Building AI Products"). Get filtered.
- Abstracts that don't take a position. Committees pick clarity over hedging.
