# Speaker Differentiation

## What it is
The four pillars that differentiate Riché as a speaker from the broader pool of AI/PM voices:

1. **Live demo capability** — can demo the Context Layer Engine live on stage. Almost no other PM voice can do this.
2. **Practitioner not theorist** — has shipped real AI products (Helm Labs, Suzy, IBM). Not a commentator.
3. **Cognitive science backing** — substantive grounding in information theory and cognitive science. Not vibes-based.
4. **Specific metrics from real products** — 300% growth, $0 CAC, $3.25M pipeline, 350+ enterprise customers.

## Why it matters
Segment 4 organizers see hundreds of speaker pitches. The differentiation pillars are how Riché's pitch survives the first 30-second filter. Generic AI/PM speakers are filtered en masse; "live demo + practitioner + cognitive science backing + specific metrics" is the bundle that doesn't appear in 99% of pitches. The differentiation is what closes Segment 4.

## How to apply
- **Use all four pillars in the speaker one-sheet.** Each gets a section: hook (live demo), credentials (practitioner), substance (cognitive science), proof (metrics).
- **Vary which pillar leads, depending on audience.** AI Product Summit audience: lead with live demo. Mind the Product PM audience: lead with practitioner credentials. Technical/founder audience: lead with cognitive science backing.
- **Don't drop pillars that feel "less relevant."** All four signal a spike of differentiation. Dropping any flattens the profile.
- **Each pillar has its own corpus entry** — see `corpus/growth/hooks/` for individual hook detail.

## Examples
- A CFP for Mind the Product opens with the live-demo capability (most differentiated), follows with practitioner credentials (Helm Labs + Suzy), grounds in cognitive science (information theory backing), closes with specific metrics ($3.25M pipeline). Four pillars, one page.
- A podcast pitch for Lenny's Podcast leans on practitioner credentials first ("I shipped 350+ enterprise customers at Suzy") because Lenny's audience trusts practitioners. Demo and metrics follow.
- A pitch for AI Product Summit leads with the live demo and the cognitive science backing because the audience is technical AI PMs who trust technical depth.

## Anti-patterns
- Picking 1–2 pillars and dropping the others. The bundle is the differentiation; partial bundles flatten.
- Soft-pedaling the metrics ("various AI products" instead of "$3.25M pipeline at Helm Labs"). Specifics are the proof.
