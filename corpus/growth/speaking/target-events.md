# Target Speaking Events

## What it is
The priority list of speaking events for Segment 4 outreach:

1. **ProductTank NYC** (June 22) — peer Segment 2 audience, NYC home turf, organic referral path.
2. **Mind the Product** (Oct 6-7) — international PM conference, CFP-driven, large stage.
3. **AI Product Summit** — AI-specific PM summit, hook-aligned with context architecture positioning.
4. **Product-Led Summit** — product-led growth slant, less AI-specific but Segment 1/2 audience.
5. **CPO Summit** — VP/CPO-level audience (Segment 1 directly), highest-leverage stage.

Plus secondary tier: SaaStr, Gartner Marketing Symposium, Product World, internal AI summits at Forbes AI 50 companies (private).

## Why it matters
Speaking is the highest-leverage activity per hour in the entire system once recorded-content reuse is included. But not all stages are equal. ProductTank NYC reaches Segment 2 directly and feeds the NYC peer network. CPO Summit reaches Segment 1 directly. AI Product Summit aligns the hook with the audience expectation. Mind the Product is the credibility marker for future pitches. Tiering the events lets the limited speaker-pitch budget flow to the highest-leverage stages first.

## How to apply
- **Pitch Tier 1 events first.** ProductTank NYC, Mind the Product, AI Product Summit. Use the CFP and pitch templates from `corpus/growth/speaking/cfp-template-structure.md` and `corpus/networking/outreach/speaker-pitch.md`.
- **Submit CFPs early.** Major conferences close CFP windows 4–6 months in advance.
- **Use Segment 2 referral pull for invitation-driven events.** ProductTank organizers ask peers; if Segment 2 work is strong, invitations come inbound.
- **Always record.** See `corpus/growth/flywheel/recorded-content-fuel.md` — the recording compounds.
- **Track outcomes.** CFP submissions, acceptances, recorded-content output per stage. Patterns reveal which stages convert to derivative content best.

## Examples
- ProductTank NYC, June 22: Riché speaks. The talk is recorded; produces 8 LinkedIn clips over 2 months. The talk also pulls 3 inbound podcast pitches from event attendees.
- Mind the Product, Oct 6-7 (target): CFP submitted in Q1 with the live-demo hook. Acceptance is uncertain but the submission itself signals seriousness for Segment 4.
- AI Product Summit (target): hook-aligned with context architecture. Submission emphasizes the live demo and the Helm Labs $3.25M pipeline credential.
- CPO Summit: high-leverage but invitation-only. Build Segment 2 referral pull until an organizer DMs.
