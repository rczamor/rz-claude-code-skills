# Specific Metrics from Real Products

## What it is
The fourth differentiation pillar: every credibility marker is a specific number from a real product, not a generic claim. Specifically:

- **Helm Labs:** $3.25M pipeline. 300% growth. $0 CAC.
- **Suzy:** 350+ enterprise customers. Launch + scale phases.
- **IBM AI Research:** technical depth, production AI systems.

The pattern: number + product + context. Never "led growth at multiple companies"; always "$3.25M pipeline at Helm Labs in 18 months."

## Why it matters
Specifics survive scrutiny; generics don't. A Segment 1 hiring manager scanning the LinkedIn profile in 30 seconds spots the difference between "$3.25M pipeline" (concrete, verifiable, impressive) and "drove growth across product portfolio" (vague, defensive, ignored). Specific metrics are also the language Segment 4 organizers and podcast bookers need to verify credibility — they can fact-check, name-drop, and cite the numbers in their own internal pitches.

## How to apply
- **Use specifics on every conversion surface:** LinkedIn About, speaker one-sheet, CFP submissions, podcast pitches, the home page of richezamor.com.
- **Pair the number with the product and the context.** "$3.25M pipeline at Helm Labs in 18 months" — number, product, context. All three.
- **Don't recycle the same metric in every post.** Friday story content rotates: one week the Helm Labs pipeline mechanics, another the Suzy launch context drift, another IBM technical depth. Variety prevents staleness.
- **Update specifics quarterly.** As work evolves, the credibility markers should evolve. The Helm Labs $3.25M number is current; if a new product role produces new metrics, integrate them.

## Examples
- LinkedIn headline: "AI Product Leader | Context Architecture | $3.25M pipeline at Helm Labs | 350+ enterprise customers at Suzy." Specifics anchor the position.
- A Wednesday deep dive opens with: "When we launched Suzy with 350+ enterprise customers, our early disengagement read like PMF failure. It was context drift." Specific number frames the lesson.
- A CFP submission to AI Product Summit: bio leads with "$3.25M pipeline at Helm Labs (300% growth, $0 CAC) and 350+ enterprise customers at Suzy." Numbers carry the credibility.
- A peer asks "what's the strongest credibility marker?" Riché picks based on audience: founders → $3.25M pipeline; PM peers → 350+ enterprise customers; technical/founders → IBM AI Research depth.

## Anti-patterns
- Generic phrasing ("led growth at multiple AI products"). Loses Segment 1's 30-second scan.
- Inflating numbers. Specifics only work if they're verifiable; over-claiming is reputation poison.
