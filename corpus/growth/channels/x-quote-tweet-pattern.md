# X Quote-Tweet Pattern

## What it is
On X, quote-tweet with added perspective rather than just retweeting. A bare retweet is invisible to your audience — it shows up in their feed as the original post. A quote tweet with substance shows your perspective and pulls your audience back into your voice. Quote-tweets are the X analog of substantive LinkedIn comments.

## Why it matters
X's algorithm and norms reward voice. A bare retweet says "I agree" without anything new — invisible to your audience, invisible to the original poster, invisible to algorithms. A quote-tweet with a sharp take pulls your audience into the original, gets noticed by the original poster (thanks to the quote-tweet notification), and trains the algorithm on your voice + topic combinations.

## How to apply
- **Default to quote-tweet over retweet.** Only retweet if the bare share itself is the message (rare).
- **Add perspective in the quote.** Same substance rule as LinkedIn comments — see `corpus/growth/playbook/comment-substance-rule.md`. Build, refine, name a tension, add a proof point.
- **Length: 1–3 sentences in the quote.** X's quote-tweet attention budget is short.
- **Engage the quoted poster.** Quote-tweets notify the original poster; that's the relationship-building leverage.
- **Daily target:** 1–2 quote-tweets in the 15 min/day X block.

## Examples
- A Shreyas Doshi tweet on PM frameworks. Bare retweet: invisible. Right: quote with "What's missing for AI PMs is that the 'framework selection' question changes when context architecture changes. The default frame doesn't apply when the user can't articulate the context they need."
- A Lenny Rachitsky tweet about PMF. Quote: "PMF for AI products has a hidden floor: context fit. Most AI products converge on UX before they've stabilized retrieval — early disengagement looks like PMF failure when it's context drift."
- A John Cutler tweet on team metrics. Quote: "The pattern holds for AI teams with one twist: most useful AI metric is invisible — context surface area, not feature output. Throughput stops being the right number."
