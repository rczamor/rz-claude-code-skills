# LinkedIn Articles (Long-Form)

## What it is
LinkedIn's long-form publishing surface — articles of 1,000–3,000+ words that live on the LinkedIn domain. Distinct from posts (300–1,300 char). Articles serve two purposes: (1) deep dives on framework-level topics, (2) SEO via LinkedIn's domain authority for terms like "context architecture," "AI context management."

## Why it matters
LinkedIn's domain authority means articles rank in Google search for relevant terms — without the SEO investment of a personal site. An article on "context architecture for AI products" published on LinkedIn can rank in Google's first page faster than the same content on richezamor.com. That ranking attracts Segment 1 and Segment 4 organically (people searching the topic), which complements the active flywheel.

## How to apply
- **Cadence:** 1 article per month. Don't overdo it — articles are a bigger time investment than posts.
- **Topic selection:** framework-level pieces that deserve more space than a 1,300-char post (the "What is context architecture?" canonical piece, "The 5 mistakes AI PMs make in their first 90 days," etc.).
- **SEO targets:** lead with the keywords from `corpus/growth/channels/website-seo-targets.md` (context architecture, AI context management, context generation AI).
- **Format:** clear H2 sections, short paragraphs, scannable. LinkedIn's article reader is mobile-first.
- **Eventually migrate to richezamor.com.** Articles can be republished to the personal site after 60–90 days for permanent SEO equity. LinkedIn keeps the snapshot; the canonical version becomes richezamor.com.

## Examples
- A Wednesday LinkedIn deep dive on context architecture grew to 1,800 words during the Sunday session. Riché publishes it as an article instead of a post. The article ranks in Google's top 5 for "context architecture for AI products" within 3 weeks.
- An article on "Why most AI PM job descriptions miss the context layer" gets reshared by 4 senior PMs and pulls 2 inbound DMs from event organizers. Doubles as Segment 4 inbound.
- After 60 days, the article is republished to richezamor.com with a link to the LinkedIn version. Personal site picks up the SEO over time; LinkedIn keeps initial reach.

## Anti-patterns

- Treating articles as repurposed Twitter threads. Articles need their own structure — H2 sections, scannable paragraphs.
