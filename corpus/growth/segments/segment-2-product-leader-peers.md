# Segment 2 — Product Leader Peers

## What it is
The PRIMARY engagement segment. Roughly 5,000–10,000 senior PMs to VPs working at AI-adjacent or tech companies. They follow Lenny Rachitsky, Shreyas Doshi, Melissa Perri, Marty Cagan, Teresa Torres. They're in Reforge Slacks, ProductTank meetups, and run their own product teams. They reshare, comment substantively, and refer peers. They are the engine of the entire growth flywheel.

## Why it matters
Without Segment 2, content stays in a small echo chamber. Segment 2 is the bridge that makes Segment 1 reach possible (peer reshares hit hiring-manager feeds), Segment 4 referrals possible (peers recommend speakers to event organizers), and Segment 3 trust possible (founders trust senior PM voices when evaluating advisors). Every other segment is reachable only through Segment 2 or alongside it. The growth strategy is fundamentally: be the most substantive voice in this group's conversation.

## How to apply
- Spend the largest share of weekly time (15–20 min/day commenting plus 2-hour Sunday writing) on Segment 2 surfaces — see `corpus/growth/playbook/budget-allocation.md`.
- Pull substantive comments onto posts by the 18 LinkedIn target accounts (Lenny, Shreyas, Melissa, Cagan, Aakash Gupta, John Cutler, etc. — see `corpus/growth/target-accounts/linkedin.yaml`).
- Show up at ProductTank NYC and similar in-person meetups. Real-world reinforcement compounds peer trust faster than any algorithm.
- Engage in Reforge Slacks and similar peer communities. Public posting is one half; community presence is the other.
- Track Segment 2 reach via reshares from 1K+ follower accounts, peer DMs, and ProductTank connections (see `corpus/growth/segments/segment-2-metrics.md`).

## Examples
- Riché comments on a Lenny Rachitsky post about PMF metrics with the context-architecture angle: "PMF for AI products is downstream of context fit, not just product-market fit." The comment lands; another senior PM with 8K followers replies. The exchange surfaces in their networks.
- A ProductTank NYC connection becomes a quarterly coffee. Six months later, that PM is now a VP and refers Riché for a head-of-product role at her new company. Segment 2 → Segment 1 conversion.
- A Reforge Slack thread on AI PMing pulls in Riché's perspective. Three peers DM after. Two become long-term relationships; one reshares his next deep dive to a 5K-follower audience.

## Anti-patterns
- Treating Segment 2 like a follower count to grow. They're peers; talk like one. The "guru speaking down to PMs" voice repels this segment instantly.
- Skipping in-person events because LinkedIn feels efficient. ProductTank handshakes compound faster than a quarter of online comments.
