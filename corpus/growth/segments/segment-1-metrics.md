# Segment 1 Metrics

## What it is
The four leading-indicator proxies for Segment 1 (AI Product Hiring Managers) reach. Because Segment 1 doesn't consume content, you can't measure them via likes or comments. You measure them through downstream signals that suggest a hiring manager has noticed.

The four proxies:
1. **Inbound recruiter DMs** — recruiters reaching out about AI product roles at the kinds of companies Segment 1 leads.
2. **Profile views from AI company employees** — LinkedIn Premium tracking of who's viewing the profile.
3. **"How did you hear about me" traces** — when a recruiter or hiring manager mentions a path, log it (peer reshare, podcast, conference, mutual connection).
4. **Mentions in hiring conversations** — anecdotal, hard to measure, but tracked when a peer says "I heard your name come up at [company]."

## Why it matters
Without leading indicators, Segment 1 reach is invisible until conversion (a job offer). Leading indicators let you steer mid-quarter — if recruiter DMs are flat after 60 days of consistent posting, the content is reaching peers but not converting to peer reshares that hit Segment 1's feeds. Adjust before another quarter passes.

## How to apply
- Set up LinkedIn Premium for profile-view tracking. Log AI-company-employee views weekly in the dashboard from `corpus/growth/metrics/dashboard-template.md`.
- When a recruiter DMs, ask "how did you hear about me?" Log the answer in the relationship tracker. Patterns reveal which channels are converting.
- Track quarterly: total recruiter DMs, AI-company profile views, named-trace count.
- Compare to lagging indicators (interviews, offers) once a quarter to validate which leading indicators predict conversion.

## Examples
- Q1 2026: 8 recruiter DMs, 3 traced to Lenny Rachitsky reshare of the context-architecture deep dive. Insight: peer reshares from Segment 2 thinkers convert to Segment 1 attention within 4–6 weeks.
- Q2 2026: profile views from a Forbes AI 50 company spike after a ProductTank NYC talk. The talk fed recorded clips into LinkedIn posts; one was reshared by a Segment 2 VP; her network includes the Forbes AI 50 hiring team.

## Anti-patterns
- Conflating "profile views" generally with Segment 1 attention. A profile view from a recruiter is signal; a view from a fellow PM peer is noise for this segment (but signal for Segment 2).
- Waiting for a job offer to validate Segment 1 reach. By the time the lagging indicator fires, you're a quarter behind on optimization.
