# Segment 4 Targeting Rules

## What it is
The rules for engaging Segment 4 — Event Organizers and Podcast Hosts. The core posture: direct, credentialed, hook-led. This is one of the few segments where direct outreach is correct, because Segment 4's job is to receive pitches. The rule: lead with the live-demo hook plus Segment 2 referral pull.

## Why it matters
Segment 4 reach is binary in a way other segments aren't — they say yes or no to a stage slot or podcast booking. Quality of pitch matters more than quantity. A weak pitch wastes both your reputation and a future shot at the same event. The hook ("I built a personal knowledge engine from scratch... I can demo it live on stage") is the differentiator, and it's wasted if buried under generic PM positioning.

## How to apply
- **LinkedIn presence first.** When organizers Google the name, the LinkedIn profile and pinned posts must demonstrate the differentiated positioning before the pitch arrives.
- **Direct LinkedIn DMs to specific organizers** with the hook. Reference the event by name and a specific reason it fits this stage.
- **Conference speaker forms** with the differentiated hook. Generic CFP submissions get filtered; the live-demo capability is the differentiator.
- **Podcast pitch emails** with: hook + specific angle for *that* host + proof point + suggested format.
- **Use Segment 2 referral pull.** Event organizers ask peers "who should we have on?" If three Segment 2 peers have reshared Riché in the last quarter, Segment 4 conversion accelerates.

## Examples
- Wrong: "I'd love to speak at your event about AI product management." Right: "I'd love to speak at ProductTank NYC about why context architecture, not model intelligence, is the moat in AI products. I built a personal knowledge engine from scratch to prove it — I can demo it live on stage."
- Wrong: cold pitch Lenny Rachitsky's podcast with "I'm an AI PM with thoughts." Right: pitch with the hook plus a specific angle the show hasn't covered (live demo of context architecture, with metrics from Helm Labs and Suzy).
- Wrong: rely on the LinkedIn profile alone. Right: send Segment 4 a one-sheet PDF that loads in 5 seconds and answers "is this person credible?"

## Anti-patterns
- Submitting generic CFPs. Generic submissions get filtered en masse.
- Pitching too many events at once with the same boilerplate. Each pitch should reference the specific event, audience, and why this hook fits this stage.
