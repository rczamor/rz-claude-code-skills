# Segment 2 Targeting Rules

## What it is
The rules for engaging Segment 2 — Product Leader Peers. The core posture: peer-to-peer, substance-first, in their existing channels. Specific surfaces: Reforge Slacks, ProductTank meetups, comment threads on Lenny Rachitsky / Shreyas Doshi / Marty Cagan / Melissa Perri / Teresa Torres / Aakash Gupta posts.

## Why it matters
Segment 2 is the engine. If the posture is wrong (guru voice, candidate voice, sales voice), every other segment downstream of this one suffers. Peers reshare peers; they don't reshare aspiring gurus. The rules below are how to hold a peer posture even when the temptation is to flex credentials.

## How to apply
- **Comment-first, not post-first.** Substantive comments on Segment 2 thinkers' posts reach more peers than your own posts in the early stages. See `corpus/growth/playbook/strategic-commenting.md`.
- **Add a perspective, never agreement.** "Great post!" is invisible. A 2-3 sentence comment that names what's missing or builds on the argument earns peer attention.
- **Show up at ProductTank NYC, Mind the Product, Reforge events.** In-person trust compounds 10x faster than digital.
- **Engage in peer Slack communities (Reforge, Lenny's, etc.).** Lurking is for Segment 4 channels; participation is for Segment 2.
- **Refer peers to peers.** When you see a great PM post, tag the right person to read it. Connection brokering is high-trust signal.

## Examples
- Wrong: Riché posts "10 Things Every PM Should Know About AI" hoping Segment 2 follows. Result: low engagement, candidate-voice signal.
- Right: Riché comments on a Shreyas post about prioritization with the context-architecture angle ("Most AI PMs prioritize features when they should prioritize context surface area"), and the comment thread becomes a 2-day conversation with three other senior PMs.
- Wrong: connect-and-pitch DM to a senior PM. Right: comment on her post, exchange three substantive replies, then DM with a connection note that references the exchange.

## Anti-patterns
- Pitching products, services, or candidacy in Segment 2 channels. Segment 2 sniffs sales voice instantly and tunes out.
- Treating ProductTank as a networking grind. Show up to learn from peers; relationships happen as a byproduct.
