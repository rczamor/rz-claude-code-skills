# Segment 1 Targeting Rules

## What it is
The hard rules for engaging (or not engaging) Segment 1 — VP/CPO-level AI product hiring managers. The core rule: never reach Segment 1 directly. They are reached only through Segments 2 (peer reshares, peer referrals) and 3 (founder advisory, where founders refer up to investors and VPs).

## Why it matters
Segment 1 is the conversion target, and conversion targets are precisely where credibility is most fragile. A direct DM from a stranger reads as candidate noise. A peer reshare with a substantive comment reads as social proof. The first costs reputation; the second buys it. Treating Segment 1 as a content-consumption audience inverts the funnel and produces the wrong kind of attention.

## How to apply
- Filter every Segment 1 outreach instinct through this question: "Is there a Segment 2 or Segment 3 person who can introduce me?" If yes, ask for the intro instead of cold-pitching.
- When a Segment 1 leader does engage (a profile view, a like, a connection request), treat it as a signal — but don't escalate the relationship until they DM first or a mutual connection brokers the introduction.
- Optimize the LinkedIn About section, headline, and pinned posts for the *passive* Segment 1 reader who lands there via a peer reshare. Lead with the differentiated positioning (practitioner, live demo, specific metrics), not generic PM language.
- Reserve direct outreach for Segments 2, 3, and 4. Segment 1 outreach is always indirect.

## Examples
- A VP of Product at an AI startup likes Riché's post but doesn't comment. Wrong move: DM her with "thanks for the like, here's my deck." Right move: note the engagement, continue posting consistently, wait for her to move first or for a peer to broker.
- Riché sees a CPO at a Forbes AI 50 company is hiring. Wrong move: cold pitch. Right move: identify Segment 2 peers in that CPO's network, build relationships there, let referral pull do the work.

## Anti-patterns
- "I'll just send a quick note to introduce myself" — this is the failure mode. Quick notes from strangers don't convert hiring decisions.
- Treating profile views as invitations to escalate. A view is a signal to keep posting, not a signal to DM.
