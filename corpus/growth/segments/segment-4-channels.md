# Segment 4 Channels

## What it is
Where Segment 4 (Event Organizers and Podcast Hosts) actually receives pitches. The pattern: LinkedIn DMs (where most organizers are), conference speaker forms (CFP portals), podcast pitch emails (each podcast has its own intake), and Segment 2 peer referrals (the highest-conversion source).

## Why it matters
Segment 4 channels are concrete and findable. The risk isn't channel discovery; it's channel quality. A bad pitch in any channel wastes the slot for that event for the year. Channel choice maps to the kind of pitch: LinkedIn DMs are warmer; CFP forms are more structured; podcast pitch emails need format suggestions baked in.

## How to apply
- **LinkedIn DMs** for direct outreach to event organizers. Lead with the hook + a specific reason this fits their event. Keep under 150 words.
- **Conference speaker forms (CFPs)** for major events (Mind the Product, AI Product Summit, Product-Led Summit, CPO Summit). Use the CFP template from `corpus/growth/speaking/cfp-template-structure.md`.
- **Podcast pitch emails** for podcast bookings. Format: hook + specific angle for that show + proof point + suggested format. Template in `corpus/growth/speaking/podcast-pitch-template.md`.
- **Segment 2 peer referrals** as the highest-conversion source. Build Segment 2 first; Segment 4 inbound follows.
- **Speaker one-sheet PDF** at richezamor.com for organizers who need a 30-second credibility check.

## Examples
- LinkedIn DM to ProductTank NYC organizer: hook + "ProductTank NYC's audience is exactly the senior PM peer group I've been engaging with on LinkedIn — happy to demo the Context Layer Engine live for them." Result: speaking slot booked.
- CFP submission to AI Product Summit: title + abstract + 3 audience takeaways + bio + why-now. Hook leads the abstract.
- Podcast pitch email to Lenny's booker: hook + "your audience has been reshareing my context-architecture deep dives; the live-demo angle would be a natural fit" + format suggestion (45-min interview with optional live demo segment).

- Skipping the speaker one-sheet. When the organizer searches "Riché Zamor speaker" and finds nothing, the credibility check fails.
