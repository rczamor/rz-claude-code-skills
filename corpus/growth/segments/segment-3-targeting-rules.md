# Segment 3 Targeting Rules

## What it is
Rules for engaging Segment 3 — Seed to Series B AI founders. The core posture: practitioner peer, not consultant. Founders trust people who have shipped, not people who write think pieces. Specific channels: X primary, accelerator alumni Slacks (Techstars, Antler) secondary, YC/founder forums tertiary.

## Why it matters
Founders are the highest-skepticism audience. They've heard every consultant, advisor, and growth hacker pitch. The only thing that lands is demonstrated work — code, products, specific metrics, real shipping experience. The hook here is "I built a personal knowledge engine from scratch" plus "300% growth, $0 CAC, $3.25M pipeline at Helm Labs" — both of which signal practitioner before signaling expert.

## How to apply
- **Lead on X, not LinkedIn.** Adapt LinkedIn content for X thread format (5–7 tweets, punchier, more direct). See `corpus/growth/channels/x-thread-format.md`.
- **Engage X target accounts:** @shreaborhade, @lennysan, @joulee, @johncutlefish, @sachinrekhi, @caborojo, @gibsonbiddle, @lissijean, @ttorres. Quote-tweet with added perspective; never just retweet.
- **Show technical depth.** The Context Layer Engine demo, the retrieval architecture, specific evals — this is the substance that earns founder attention.
- **Show up in accelerator alumni networks where you have standing.** Don't cold-DM YC founders; participate in Slacks where you've been invited.
- **Refuse advisory pitches in early exchanges.** Free conversation first; equity conversations only after 2–3 substantive exchanges where the value has been demonstrated.

## Examples
- Wrong: "I help AI founders with product strategy" generic positioning. Right: "I built the Context Layer Engine from scratch — here's the retrieval architecture and the evals that proved context architecture is the moat."
- Wrong: cold DM a YC founder offering advisory. Right: respond substantively to a founder's X thread on retrieval, exchange three messages, then offer a specific resource.
- Wrong: post LinkedIn deep dives and expect founders to find them. Right: turn Wednesday LinkedIn deep dive into a Tuesday X thread; founders see X first.

## Anti-patterns
- Using LinkedIn-style guru voice on X. Founders punish that voice harder than peers do; X norms reward terse, technical, opinionated.
- Pitching paid advisory before any value has been demonstrated. Founders pattern-match to consultants and tune out.
