# Segment 2 Channels

## What it is
Where Segment 2 (Product Leader Peers) actually shows up. The pattern: LinkedIn primary, Slack communities (Reforge, Lenny's Slack, peer-curated invite-only groups) secondary, in-person meetups (ProductTank NYC, Mind the Product local chapters, Reforge events) tertiary. Newsletters from Lenny, Shreyas, Aakash. Some podcast listening.

## Why it matters
Segment 2 has a stable channel pattern, which makes them findable. They're not on Threads or Mastodon. They're on LinkedIn, in private Slacks, and at conferences. Showing up consistently in those three places is the entire surface area. Getting that channel mix wrong (e.g., investing in Medium or Threads) wastes the budget.

## How to apply
- **LinkedIn (primary):** post 3–5x/week using Mon-Fri content types. Spend 15–20 min/day commenting on Segment 2 thinkers' posts. The algorithm rewards active engagement; the audience rewards substance.
- **Slack communities (secondary):** participate in Reforge Slacks, Lenny's Slack, and any peer-curated invite-only spaces. Public-feed presence is half the work; community presence is the other half.
- **In-person meetups (tertiary, high-ROI per event):** ProductTank NYC monthly, Mind the Product (Oct 6-7), Reforge events. Real-world handshakes compound peer trust faster than 10 weeks of digital.
- **Don't bother with:** Medium (degraded distribution), Threads (consumer-skewed), Latent Space (engineering-focused, not PM).

## Examples
- A senior PM in the Reforge Slack posts about a tricky AI rollout. Riché replies with a substantive frame from his Helm Labs experience. Three peers DM after. Two become quarterly contacts.
- ProductTank NYC, June 22: Riché meets a VP of Product at an AI company. They exchange notes on context architecture. He follows up within 48 hours with a specific resource. The relationship compounds over the next two quarters.
- LinkedIn Wednesday deep dive on context architecture pulls Lenny Rachitsky's attention. Lenny reshares. Riché's profile views from senior PMs spike for 10 days.

## Anti-patterns
- Spreading thin across Medium, Substack, Threads, Mastodon. Segment 2 isn't there in numbers worth the effort.
- Skipping ProductTank because it feels less efficient than posting. In-person trust is the single highest-ROI channel for Segment 2 conversion to deeper relationships.
