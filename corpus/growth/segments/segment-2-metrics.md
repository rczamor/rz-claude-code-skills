# Segment 2 Metrics

## What it is
The four leading-indicator proxies for Segment 2 (Product Leader Peers) reach:

1. **Comment engagement from senior PMs** — substantive replies (not "great post!") from PMs at the senior+ level on Riché's posts and on his comments on others' posts.
2. **Reshares from people with 1,000+ followers** — peer-amplification signal; this is what creates Segment 1 reach.
3. **DM conversations initiated by peers** — when senior PMs reach out to discuss work, ask for advice, or share their own challenges.
4. **ProductTank connections and follow-ups** — in-person meetings that convert to ongoing relationships (LinkedIn connections, follow-up coffee, ongoing DMs).

## Why it matters
Segment 2 is the engine, so its proxies are the most actionable metrics in the system. Quality of engagement matters more than quantity: one reshare from a 5K-follower senior PM beats 50 likes from juniors. These proxies tell you whether the flywheel is actually spinning or whether you're posting into an echo chamber.

## How to apply
- Track weekly: comment engagement from senior+ PMs (count and quality), reshares from 1K+ accounts, peer DMs initiated, ProductTank/event-driven new connections.
- Distinguish peer engagement from generic engagement. A "great post!" from a junior is noise; a substantive disagreement from a VP is gold.
- Review monthly: which posts pulled the strongest peer engagement? Patterns reveal the topics, framings, and proof points that resonate with Segment 2.
- Correlate with Segment 1 proxies. Strong Segment 2 reshare signal in Q1 should produce Segment 1 profile views in Q2 (typical 4–8 week lag).

## Examples
- Riché posts a Wednesday deep dive on context architecture. By day 3: 12 comments, 4 from senior PMs, 2 reshares from 2K+ follower accounts, 1 peer DM saying "this is exactly what I've been trying to articulate." Strong Segment 2 signal.
- Same week, his comment on a Lenny Rachitsky post pulls a reply from a VP at an AI company. That reply becomes a 6-message exchange and a connection. Logged as a Segment 2 conversion.
- ProductTank NYC: 4 new connections made; 2 follow up within a week; 1 becomes a quarterly coffee. Logged in CRM, tied to event ROI.

## Anti-patterns
- Counting any comment as Segment 2 engagement. Distinguish senior-PM substance from junior cheerleading.
- Treating reshares as the only proxy. Peer DMs and in-person follow-ups are equally signal.
