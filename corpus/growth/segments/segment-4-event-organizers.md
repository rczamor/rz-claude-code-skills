# Segment 4 — Event Organizers and Podcast Hosts

## What it is
The gatekeepers to stages and recorded content: ProductTank organizers, Mind the Product CFP committee, AI Product Summit programmers, Product-Led Summit team, CPO Summit committee, podcast hosts (Lenny Rachitsky, Melissa Perri, Shreyas Doshi, Teresa Torres, Petra Wille, others). Smaller in number than other segments — maybe a few dozen — but each one unlocks high-leverage exposure to Segments 1, 2, and 3 simultaneously.

## Why it matters
Speaking creates recorded content. Recorded content fuels more posts. Posts grow Segment 2 reach. Segment 2 reshares hit Segment 1. Segment 4 sits at the top of an entire fork of the flywheel. Reaching Segment 4 directly (with the live-demo hook) is one of the few cases where direct outreach is the right move, because Segment 4's job is literally to receive pitches.

## How to apply
- Use the differentiated speaker hook: "I built a personal knowledge engine from scratch to prove that context architecture, not model intelligence, is the real moat in AI products. I can demo it live on stage." See `corpus/growth/speaking/the-hook.md`.
- Reach Segment 4 directly via LinkedIn DMs, conference speaker forms, and podcast pitch emails. The full pitch templates live in `corpus/networking/outreach/speaker-pitch.md`.
- Build Segment 2 referral pull. Event organizers ask peers "who should we have on?" Strong Segment 2 signal becomes Segment 4 inbound.
- Maintain a current speaker one-sheet and bio page on richezamor.com. When a Segment 4 organizer Googles "Riché Zamor speaker," the materials should be 30 seconds of work to verify.
- Track Segment 4 reach via speaking invitations (inbound), CFP acceptances, podcast guest requests, and event organizer DMs.

## Examples
- ProductTank NYC organizer DMs after a Segment 2 peer mentions Riché. The hook plus the Helm Labs $3.25M pipeline credibility marker plus the Suzy launch credential closes the speaking slot in one exchange.
- Lenny Rachitsky's podcast booker reaches out via the PM Slack network. Riché's deep dive on context architecture had been reshared by 3 senior PMs in Lenny's audience. The pitch references the live demo capability.
- A CFP submitted to AI Product Summit gets accepted on the strength of the practitioner-not-theorist positioning + specific metrics from Helm Labs.

## Anti-patterns
- Pitching events with generic AI/PM angles. "I can talk about AI product management" lands nowhere; "I can demo a live context architecture engine on stage" lands.
- Skipping the speaker one-sheet because "the LinkedIn profile is enough." Organizers need a 30-second answer to "is this person credible?"
