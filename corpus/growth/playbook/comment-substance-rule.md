# Comment Substance Rule

## What it is
Every comment adds a perspective, not agreement. Never write "Great post!" or "100% agree" or "This is gold." A comment without a perspective is invisible to the post owner, invisible to other readers, and a wasted minute of the daily commenting block.

## Why it matters
The strategic commenting tactic works because peers notice substance over time. Substance is what makes a senior PM remember the commenter and engage back. Agreement is what makes the comment indistinguishable from the 50 others on the post. The rule is the difference between the tactic compounding and the tactic being noise.

## How to apply
A substantive comment does one or more of the following:
- **Builds on the frame** with a specific extension or refinement.
- **Names a tension** the post implies but doesn't address.
- **Adds a proof point** from your own work (Helm Labs, Suzy, IBM, Context Layer Engine) that supports or complicates the post.
- **Offers a counter-perspective** with respect — "Where I'd push back is..." not "Wrong, here's why."
- **References a related thinker** when genuine — "This pairs with the Kahneman 'fast/slow' frame because..."

The shape: 2–4 sentences. Specific. Voicy. Read your own comment back — does it stand alone as a useful thought, or does it only make sense in reaction?

## Examples
- Wrong: "Great frame, Lenny!"
- Right: "The frame holds — what I'd add for AI PM contexts is that 'time to value' has a hidden floor: you can't reduce TTV below the context-bootstrapping cost. Most AI products optimize the wrong half of TTV."
- Wrong: "100% on this one, Shreyas."
- Right: "Where I see this break in practice: AI teams interpret 'high-leverage' as 'high-impact features' when it's usually 'invisible context surface area.' The leverage is two layers down from where most roadmaps look."
- Wrong: "Fire."
- Right: "The Suzy launch was a forcing function for this exact mistake — we converged on UX before we'd stabilized the retrieval surface, and our early disengagement looked like PMF failure. It was context drift."

## Anti-patterns
- Long, detailed comments that hijack the post. Stay 2–4 sentences; respect the host's airtime.
- Generic "agreed, important point" comments. They do nothing for relationship building or reach.
