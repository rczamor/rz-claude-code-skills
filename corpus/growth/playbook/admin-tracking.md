# Admin and Tracking (15 min/week)

## What it is
The 15-minute weekly review block. Three tasks: (1) update the metrics dashboard, (2) update the relationship CRM, (3) plan the next week's content topics. Friday afternoon is the natural slot — close the week before the Sunday writing session.

## Why it matters
Without a tracking ritual, the system runs on intuition. Intuition is wrong about what's working — most personal-brand operators overestimate their reach and underestimate their relationship gaps. The 15 min/week is a forcing function for honest measurement, and the inputs feed both the Sunday writing session and the quarterly review.

## How to apply
- **Update the dashboard (5 min).** Log this week's: posts published, total comments received, reshares from 1K+ accounts, peer DMs initiated, profile views. Use the template from `corpus/growth/metrics/dashboard-template.md`.
- **Update relationship CRM (5 min).** New connections made, comment exchanges that converted to DMs, follow-ups due next week. See `corpus/networking/` for the CRM canon.
- **Plan next week's topics (5 min).** Review the "next post topics" doc and pre-select Mon-Fri topics for the Sunday session. Don't draft yet — just topic-level.

Friday close-of-week is the natural anchor. Block it on the calendar.

## Examples
- Friday 4:00 PM: Riché updates the dashboard. Reshares jumped this week (3 from 2K+ accounts) — patterns suggest the Wednesday deep dive on context architecture pulled. Logged. CRM shows 2 new senior-PM connections from comment exchanges; one needs a follow-up next week. Content topics for the upcoming Sunday session: Monday hot take on AI PM JD's, Wednesday deep dive on retrieval architecture, Friday story on Suzy launch.
- A bad week: dashboard skipped two weeks running. Intuition says "things are great"; data shows reshares flat for 3 weeks. Diagnostic kicks in: relationship gap with Segment 2.

## Anti-patterns
- Skipping admin "because nothing changed." Skipping is how the relationship gap goes undetected for two months.
- Letting admin balloon to 45 min. The 15 min cap is the discipline; if admin needs more, automate or batch quarterly.
