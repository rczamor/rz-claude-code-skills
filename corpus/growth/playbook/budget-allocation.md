# Weekly Time Budget — 5.25 Hours

## What it is
The total weekly time allocation for all growth activities. 5.25 hours per week, broken down into:

- **Strategic commenting on LinkedIn:** 15–20 min/day × 7 days ≈ 1.75–2.3 hrs/week (target: ~1.75 hrs)
- **DM relationship building:** 2–3 DMs per week × ~10 min each ≈ 0.5 hrs/week
- **Batch writing (Sunday):** 2 hrs in one session, all 5 posts for the week
- **X cross-posting + engagement:** 15 min/day × 5 weekdays ≈ 1.25 hrs/week
- **Admin / tracking / planning:** 15 min/week

Total: ~5.25 hours.

## Why it matters
The budget is a discipline tool. Without it, growth activities expand to fill available time and crowd out the higher-leverage work (PM work, AI Researcher routine, paid engagements). Personal-brand work has unlimited demand for time; this budget sets the supply ceiling. Anything that doesn't fit gets refused, not absorbed.

## How to apply
- Block the time in calendar each week:
  - Daily 15–20 min commenting block (LinkedIn)
  - Daily 15 min X engagement block
  - Sunday 2-hour writing block
  - Friday 15 min admin block
  - 30 min/week reserved for DMs (split across the week as opportunities arise)
- Refuse activities that don't fit. Newsletter? Deferred to Q3 2026 because it doesn't fit the budget. Medium? Excluded entirely.
- Re-balance quarterly. If Segment 4 inbound is rising and speaking is taking off, the recorded-content reuse work shifts hours away from raw posting.
- Honor the budget under pressure. Big content opportunities don't justify ballooning to 10 hours/week — they justify reallocating within 5.25.

## Examples
- A peer asks Riché to co-author a deep dive that would take 4 hours. The full week's writing budget is 2 hours. He declines or scopes down.
- A new podcast pitch comes in mid-week. He triages: is this a Tier 1 podcast? If yes, it consumes the next Sunday writing block. If no, defer or refuse.
- The ProductTank NYC talk in June requires prep. He absorbs the prep into the Sunday block for two weeks, deferring 2 LinkedIn posts (Tuesday signal posts).

## Anti-patterns
- Letting any single activity eat the budget. If commenting takes 45 min/day, kill the lowest-leverage 25 min.
- Adding new channels without removing existing ones. Newsletter without dropping something else = budget overrun.
