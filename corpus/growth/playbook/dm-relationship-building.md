# DM Relationship Building (2–3/week)

## What it is
2–3 direct messages per week on LinkedIn, sent to peers (Segment 2) when something they've posted maps to Riché's work or vice versa. Connect, don't pitch. The full template canon lives in `corpus/networking/outreach/`. This entry is the *rate, posture, and decision rule* for when to send a DM.

## Why it matters
DMs are the relationship-deepening tactic that converts comment exchanges into longer-term peer connections. Done well, 2–3 DMs per week compounds into 100+ active peer relationships per year — the engine of Segment 2 reach. Done poorly (cold pitches, generic "love your content" notes), DMs damage reputation and burn future opportunities at the same accounts.

## How to apply
- **Trigger:** send a DM when (a) someone posted something that genuinely maps to Riché's work, (b) you've exchanged 2–3 substantive comments first, or (c) you have a specific resource that adds value (an article, an intro, a reference).
- **Posture:** connect, don't pitch. Reference the specific post or exchange. Offer something or ask something specific. Not "let's chat" — that's wasted air.
- **Length:** 3–5 sentences. Anything longer reads as cold pitch.
- **Cadence:** 2–3 DMs per week. Not 10. Volume signals desperation; selectivity signals trust-worthiness.
- **Track responses.** When a DM converts to an exchange, log the relationship in CRM (see `corpus/networking/`). When it doesn't, note pattern; don't follow up immediately.

## Examples
- Senior PM at an AI company posts about retrieval challenges. Riché has commented twice in the past month, substantively. He DMs: "Your post on retrieval drift mapped exactly to a problem we hit at Suzy. I wrote up the diagnostic we used — happy to send it if useful." Specific, valuable, no pitch.
- A peer reshares Riché's Wednesday deep dive. Riché DMs to thank her, references something specific from her recent post, and asks one substantive question about her current work. Connection deepens.
- A founder DMs Riché after a thread; Riché replies same-day, references the founder's last post specifically, and offers a 15-min call. The DM converts to advisory.

## Anti-patterns
- Cold DMs without prior comment exchange. Reads as candidate or sales pitch.
- "Let's connect, would love to chat" — vague DMs waste both sides' time.
