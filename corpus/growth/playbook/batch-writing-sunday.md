# Batch Writing — 2-Hour Sunday Session

## What it is
All 5 posts for the week written in one 2-hour Sunday session. Monday hot take, Tuesday signal, Wednesday deep dive, Thursday framework, Friday story. The full content type canon lives in `corpus/content-system/`. This entry covers the *batch workflow* — why batching, how to structure the 2 hours, what to bring into the session.

## Why it matters
Batching solves three problems at once: (1) it caps content time at 2 hrs/week, (2) it produces consistent voice across the week (one writing session, one voice state), (3) it eliminates daily decision fatigue ("what should I post today?"). Without batching, content time inflates and quality degrades as the week wears on.

## How to apply
- **Sunday block, 2 hours.** Same time each week. Treat as sacred; protect from meetings.
- **Bring inputs in advance.** Throughout the week, save signals (interesting articles, peer threads, observations) to a "next post topics" doc. The Sunday session draws from that pool.
- **Order: easiest first.** Friday story (a known anecdote) often takes 15 min. Wednesday deep dive (a framework) takes 45 min. Tuesday signal (a quick reaction) takes 10 min. Tackle the easy ones first to build momentum.
- **Use the content type canon.** Each day has a defined type with structure expectations — see `corpus/content-system/` for Mon-Fri formats.
- **Voice rules apply.** See `corpus/voice/`. No emojis, no Fatal Fifteen phrases.
- **Schedule directly.** Use LinkedIn's native scheduling (or direct API) — see `corpus/growth/anti-patterns/no-buffer-typefully.md`.

## Examples
- Sunday 9–11 AM: Riché writes Monday's hot take on AI PM job descriptions, Tuesday's signal post quoting a Cagan article, Wednesday's deep dive on context architecture (45 min — the heavy one), Thursday's framework for AI feature prioritization, and Friday's story about the Suzy launch context drift moment. Schedules all 5. Done.
- A bad week: Sunday session runs 3.5 hours because Wednesday's deep dive grew. Diagnostic: the deep dive should have been narrower. Constraint forces sharper writing next week.
- A good week: Sunday session ends at 1:45 because two posts were drafted earlier from a podcast Riché was on. Recorded-content fuel feeding the batch.

## Anti-patterns
- Drafting daily instead of batching. Time inflates; voice degrades.
- Letting the Sunday session run over 2 hours. The constraint is the discipline.
