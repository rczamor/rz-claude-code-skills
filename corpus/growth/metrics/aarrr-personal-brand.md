# AARRR (Pirate Metrics) Adapted to Personal Brand

## What it is
Dave McClure's AARRR framework — Acquisition, Activation, Retention, Referral, Revenue — adapted to a personal-brand growth context. The original framework was designed for product/SaaS growth. The adapted personal-brand version maps each stage to Riché's flywheel:

- **Acquisition (Discovery):** how someone first encounters Riché. Leading indicators: profile views, X impressions on threads, search rankings on richezamor.com.
- **Activation (Connection):** the first meaningful engagement. Leading indicators: substantive comment exchange, follow, X reply, demo URL click.
- **Retention (DM/Continued Engagement):** sustained engagement over weeks. Leading indicators: peer DMs initiated, comment exchanges across multiple posts, return visits to richezamor.com.
- **Referral:** existing peers referring others. Leading indicators: reshares from 1K+ accounts, peer recommendations to event organizers, "told a friend about your work" reports.
- **Revenue (Career Conversion):** the lagging outcome. Indicators: recruiter DMs, advisory inquiries, speaking invitations accepted, job offers.

## Why it matters
McClure's framework — originally published in 2007 ("Startup Metrics for Pirates") — is the canonical funnel structure for SaaS. Adapting it for personal brand makes each stage of the flywheel measurable in a familiar shape. It also reveals where Riché's funnel leaks. A leak at Activation (lots of profile views, no follows) is different from a leak at Referral (lots of follows, no reshares); each leak gets a different fix.

## How to apply
- **Map each segment proxy to an AARRR stage.** See `corpus/growth/metrics/segment-proxies-rollup.md`.
- **Diagnose the funnel quarterly.** Where's the biggest leak? Acquisition gap = SEO + content reach issue. Activation gap = profile or hook issue. Referral gap = relationship + reshare-mechanics issue.
- **Don't over-engineer.** This is a heuristic frame, not a strict measurement system. Riché's actual primary metrics remain the segment proxies.
- **Cite the canonical source** when explaining the frame to others: Dave McClure, "Startup Metrics for Pirates" (2007).

## Examples
- Q1 2026 funnel diagnosis: Acquisition strong (profile views up 30%); Activation weak (follows from profile views ~15%, below benchmark). Diagnostic: profile hook isn't converting curious visitors to followers. Fix: refine LinkedIn About section, add stronger CTA to Featured demo.
- Q2 2026: Referral gap. Plenty of likes and follows; reshares from 1K+ accounts flat. Diagnostic: Segment 2 relationships not deepening. Fix: prioritize comment substance and 2–3 DMs/week to specific senior PMs.
- The full funnel chain: A senior PM finds Riché via Lenny reshare (Acquisition) → reads pinned posts and follows (Activation) → engages on next 3 Wednesday deep dives over 6 weeks (Retention) → reshares one to her audience (Referral) → her CPO sees the reshare → recruiter DM follows (Revenue).

## Anti-patterns
- Treating Revenue as the only meaningful metric. A funnel approach exists precisely because earlier stages are the leading indicators.
- Adopting AARRR with strict thresholds. Personal brand isn't SaaS; the frame is heuristic.
