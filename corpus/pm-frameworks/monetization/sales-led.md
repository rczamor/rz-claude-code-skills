# Sales-Led Growth

## What it is
A GTM motion in which a sales team drives acquisition through outbound prospecting, demos, custom proposals, and contract negotiation. The buyer is typically an enterprise procurement-level decision maker who is *not* a hands-on user. Deal cycles are long (3–18 months), ACVs are high ($50K–$5M+), and the playbook involves outbound SDRs, AE-led discovery, sales engineers, and procurement legal. The opposite GTM motion to PLG.

## Why it matters
Sales-led is the right motion for products where (a) the buyer is not the user (CIO buys, IT admin uses), (b) the deal size justifies a sales team's cost, (c) the product requires complex configuration, integration, or compliance work, or (d) the market is too small for self-serve scale. Most enterprise B2B over $50K ACV runs sales-led, even when wrapped in PLG marketing language.

## How to apply
1. **Build a target account list.** Sales-led starts with a defined ICP (ideal customer profile) and named accounts, not a wide-net signup form.
2. **Outbound + inbound.** SDRs do outbound prospecting; marketing supplies inbound from content, events, ads. Both feed AE-owned pipelines.
3. **Run a defined sales process.** MEDDIC, MEDDPICC, or similar qualification frameworks. Stages tracked in CRM.
4. **Sales engineers carry the technical weight.** Demos, POCs, technical objections answered by SEs. Lets AEs focus on commercial aspects.
5. **Customer success post-sale.** Onboarding, renewal, expansion all owned by CSM team. Sales-led products live or die on renewal mechanics.

## Examples
1. **Salesforce's classic sales-led model** — outbound + inbound + AE-owned territories + sales engineers + heavy partner channel. The reference architecture for enterprise SaaS sales motion.
2. **Riché's Helm Labs $3.25M pre-launch pipeline** is a sales-led validation outcome — the buyer-side pipeline was built through direct outreach, qualification, and discovery, not self-serve.
3. **Riché's Suzy with 350+ enterprise customers** — sales-led motion at the enterprise tier; AEs and CSMs own the relationship even as the product itself enables PLG-style expansion within the account.

## Anti-patterns
1. **Sales-led for an SMB product.** ACVs too small to justify the sales team's cost. The unit economics don't work — burn through investors' money on dial-for-dollars.
2. **PLG marketing on a sales-led product.** Self-serve signup forms that funnel into "schedule a demo" with no actual self-serve path. Users get angry; conversions tank.
