# Product-Led Growth (PLG)

## What it is
A go-to-market motion in which the product itself drives acquisition, activation, expansion, and retention — instead of (or as a complement to) outbound sales. Term popularized by OpenView Venture Partners (Blake Bartlett, ~2016). The user is also the buyer in many PLG flows, or the user becomes the internal champion who triggers the buyer. PLG products are typically self-serve, with strong onboarding, in-product upgrade flows, and viral or network-driven distribution.

## Why it matters
PLG dramatically lowers CAC compared to sales-led motions, makes early adoption frictionless, and produces fast feedback loops because users are signing up daily. But PLG is not a pricing model — it's a GTM motion. PLG products can monetize via freemium, usage-based, seat-based, or hybrid. The misconception that PLG = freemium is responsible for many failed attempts. PLG demands product-quality investments (onboarding, in-product education, self-serve admin) that take years to mature.

## How to apply
1. **Self-serve discovery.** Users find the product organically (search, content, referral) and can sign up without talking to sales.
2. **Time-to-value < 5 minutes.** First "aha moment" reachable inside a single session. Otherwise users churn before activating.
3. **In-product upgrade.** No call required to upgrade — credit card, billing, plan changes self-serve.
4. **Bottom-up adoption, top-down expansion.** Individuals or small teams adopt; once adoption reaches a threshold, sales engages for enterprise expansion (Slack, Notion, Figma all run this play).
5. **Instrument the PLG funnel.** Signup → activation → habit → upgrade → expansion. Each stage with conversion rates and cohorted by acquisition channel.

## Examples
1. **Slack's PLG-to-enterprise model** — bottom-up team adoption drove organic growth; once teams crossed a threshold, sales motion engaged for enterprise security/SSO/compliance expansion. ~60% of Slack's enterprise revenue followed this pattern at IPO.
2. **Figma** — self-serve sign-up, frictionless multiplayer collaboration as the viral mechanic; design teams adopted, then engineering and PM joined, then enterprise contracts. Same play.
3. **Notion's PLG** — personal free tier to team to enterprise. Each tier has a clear upgrade trigger (collaboration, admin, security).

## Anti-patterns
1. **PLG = freemium confusion.** Launching a free tier without product-quality investment in onboarding and self-serve. Users churn before activating.
2. **PLG without sales overlay.** PLG handles bottom-up; enterprise expansion still needs sales for SSO, security review, contract terms. Pure PLG ceiling is real.
