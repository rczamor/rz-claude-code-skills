# Lenny Rachitsky

## What they're known for

Lenny Rachitsky is a former Airbnb product lead and the author of Lenny's Newsletter — a weekly Substack publication that became the dominant practitioner forum in product management between 2020 and 2026. He also hosts Lenny's Podcast, runs a paid community, and co-founded the Maven cohort-based course platform. His canonical contribution is curatorial rather than original: Lenny synthesizes and amplifies the work of practitioners (Doshi, Torres, Cagan, Biddle, Mehta, etc.) through interviews, frameworks-in-practice essays, and benchmark surveys (PM compensation, growth team structure, AI tool adoption). The newsletter has tens of thousands of paid subscribers and is the primary reference layer for working PMs on day-to-day decisions.

## Why they matter to a Riché-facing skill

Lenny matters operationally, not theoretically. When Riché needs a quick benchmark — "what does PM career progression look like at top AI companies?" or "how are the best AI PMs running discovery?" — Lenny's archive is the first stop. For Riché's networking goals (the rz-networking skill), Lenny is a strategic target — being on Lenny's Podcast or in his newsletter is one of the highest-leverage distribution moves for a PM building a public profile. For Riché's content strategy, Lenny's editorial format (interview-driven, framework-forward, named practitioners) is a useful reference model.

## Where they're wrong / disagree with others

Lenny's curatorial role is unusually influential, which means his selection biases shape practitioner consensus. He tends to interview the same 50–100 practitioners repeatedly, which over time creates a homogenous "Lenny canon" that may overweight certain frameworks (Torres's OST is much more dominant in PM discourse partly because Lenny features her often). The newsletter's benchmarks — while useful — sometimes mix small samples and self-selected respondents, which can produce confident conclusions that don't generalize. Critics note that the format favors well-credentialed practitioners from name-brand companies (Stripe, Airbnb, Linear, Notion) over PMs in less glamorous domains where the work is just as rigorous. For AI product specifically, Lenny's coverage is good but lags the actual practitioner edge by 6–12 months — by the time a framework gets a Lenny interview, working AI PMs have already moved past it.
