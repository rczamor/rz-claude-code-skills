# Ravi Mehta

## What they're known for

Ravi Mehta is the former Chief Product Officer at Tinder, former product leader at Facebook and Tripadvisor, and now the founder of Outpace, a coaching firm for product leaders. His most-cited contribution is the Product Competency Model — a four-quadrant framework that maps PM capability across Product Execution, Customer Insight, Product Strategy, and Influencing People. The model is paired with leveling rubrics that describe what each competency looks like at APM, PM, Senior PM, GPM, Director, and VP/CPO levels. Mehta's adjacent work covers PM career paths, performance reviews, and how to interview for PM roles at high-bar companies.

## Why they matter to a Riché-facing skill

The Competency Model is the right tool when Riché is doing self-assessment, evaluating his team at Suzy, hiring, or thinking about his own growth path toward CPO / founder. The four-quadrant frame surfaces blind spots: many strong PMs are 9/10 on Execution and Customer Insight but 5/10 on Strategy and Influence — the model makes that asymmetry visible and actionable. For Riché specifically, Strategy and Influence are the leverage quadrants going forward; Execution and Insight are already strong. Mehta's leveling rubrics are also useful when writing job descriptions or calibrating against the Forbes AI 50 PM standard.

## Quotable / signature ideas

- "Most PMs over-index on the quadrant they're already strong in. Growth comes from the quadrants you avoid." (Outpace blog, 2022)
- "You can be a great executor and a poor strategist. The market doesn't care which one you are — it punishes the gap." (LinkedIn post, 2023)
- "Influence is not a soft skill. It's the multiplier on every other competency." (Lenny's Podcast, 2022)

## Where they're wrong / disagree with others

The Competency Model is descriptive, not predictive — it tells you where you are, not what to do next. Doshi would observe that the four quadrants don't all have equal leverage at all levels; for a senior PM, Strategy and Influence are 10x leverage, while Execution is closer to 1x. The model also undertheorizes domain depth — a PM who's deep in fintech infrastructure has different competency demands than one in consumer mobile. For AI products specifically, Mehta's model needs a fifth quadrant (or a sub-axis): Technical Fluency around models, evals, and context architecture. Without that, the model treats AI PMs as if they're solving the same problems as 2015-era PMs.
