# Escape the Build Trap

## What it is
Melissa Perri's framework from *Escaping the Build Trap* (2018). Stated verbatim in the product-management skill: "When companies measure success by outputs (features shipped) rather than outcomes (problems solved). The escape: outcome-oriented product management." The build trap is the organizational anti-pattern where success is defined as velocity — story points, releases, features per quarter — rather than the customer or business problem solved.

## Why it matters
Most companies that "do agile" ship more features without growing the business. Perri names the mechanism: when the highest-status work is shipping, teams optimize for shipping. The build trap explains why feature factories produce decreasing returns — every shipped feature must be maintained, supported, and migrated, but only a fraction move outcomes. Escaping it requires structural changes to incentives, OKRs, and roadmap rituals.

## How to apply
1. **Diagnose.** Are KPIs feature-completion rate, story points, releases? That's output. Are they retention deltas, NPS, conversion, NRR? That's outcome.
2. **Replace the roadmap format.** Move from "Q3: ship features A/B/C" to "Q3: improve activation rate from 32% to 40% — current bets are A, B, C." Bets change as discovery learns.
3. **Restructure incentives.** Reward problem-solving, not shipping. A team that ships nothing because the experiment falsified the hypothesis has done valuable work.
4. **Empower the team.** Outcome ownership requires authority to choose solutions (see Cagan's empowered teams).
5. **Communicate up.** Executives often request features by name. Translate up: "the feature you're asking for serves outcome X — here are three options ranked by expected impact."

## Examples
1. **Riché's Suzy Decision Engine** is structured around outcomes — Intelligence (insight quality), Insights (cycle time), Impact (action conversion) — not feature counts. The 350+ enterprise customer footprint validates that outcome framing scales.
2. **Amazon's "working backwards"** ritual (PR/FAQ before any code) is an anti-build-trap mechanism — teams must articulate the outcome before writing the spec.
3. **Microsoft Office's customer-obsession reset under Satya Nadella** moved KPIs from feature-shipping to engagement and retention — same teams, very different roadmap quality.

## Anti-patterns
1. **Outcome theater.** Renaming features as "outcomes" without changing what gets measured or rewarded. Teams notice immediately and revert to shipping.
2. **Outcome with no authority.** Assigning outcomes to teams that still need executive sign-off on every feature decision. Produces accountability without ability — worse than feature factory.
