# OKRs

## What it is
**Objectives and Key Results.** A goal-setting framework formalized by Andy Grove at Intel in the 1970s (*High Output Management*) and popularized at Google by John Doerr (*Measure What Matters*, 2018). Each OKR has one **Objective** (a qualitative, inspirational direction — "what we want to achieve") and 3–5 **Key Results** (quantitative, measurable outcomes that, if all hit, prove the Objective is achieved). Set quarterly or annually, cascaded loosely from company → team. Stretch by design — hitting 70% of KRs is healthy.

## Why it matters
From Grove (1971, internalized at Intel) and Doerr (codified for the broader market, 2018). OKRs solve two coupled problems: alignment (everyone running in the same direction) and measurement (knowing whether we got there). Without them, teams default to either roadmap-as-strategy (output focus, build-trap risk) or vibes (no shared definition of success). OKRs force the conversion of strategy into measurable outcomes.

## How to apply
1. **Objective: qualitative, inspirational, time-bound.** "Become the trusted insights platform for enterprise consumer brands." Not "ship 10 features."
2. **Key Results: 3–5 measurable outcomes.** Each KR should be a *result* (NRR ≥ 120%, activation ≥ 60%), not an *activity* (run 3 marketing campaigns).
3. **Cascade loosely, not linearly.** Team OKRs ladder up to company OKRs but aren't mechanical breakdowns. Teams negotiate ownership of company-level KRs.
4. **Score quarterly.** 0.7 average is the target — if you're hitting 1.0, your OKRs are too soft.
5. **Decouple from compensation.** Tying bonuses to OKR scores destroys ambition. Use them to align direction; reward against separate performance criteria.
6. **Update if reality changes.** OKRs are a forecast; if a market shift invalidates a KR, replace it. Don't grade against fiction.

## Examples
1. **Google's foundational OKRs** under Larry Page included "Organize the world's information and make it universally accessible and useful" with KRs around index size, query latency, and search-result relevance. Scaled to thousands of teams.
2. **Riché's Suzy Decision Engine** OKRs likely include Objectives around "Compress insights cycle time for enterprise teams" with KRs around time-to-insight, customer activation, and NRR — each measurable and decoupled from feature output.
3. **Spotify's quarterly OKRs** at the squad level publish internally — example: Objective "Increase weekly active podcast listeners," KRs around DAU/MAU ratio, retention by cohort, and per-listener listening minutes.

## Anti-patterns
1. **KRs that are activities, not outcomes.** "Launch onboarding redesign" is an activity. "Increase activation from 32% to 50%" is an outcome.
2. **Tied to compensation.** When bonuses depend on OKR scores, teams sandbag. Stretch dies. Use them for alignment only.
3. **Cascading mechanically.** Treating every team OKR as a forced subset of a company OKR. Teams lose ownership of their problem space.
