# Network Activation — Dormant

## What it is
The reconnect message sent to dormant network contacts — Techstars alumni, Antler peers, IBM colleagues, Hill Holliday and Phase2 contacts — who already know Riché but haven't been in active contact for months or years. The relationship trust exists. The current context doesn't. This template re-establishes context fast and offers a hook for re-engagement.

## Why it matters
Dormant networks are the highest-ROI cold-feeling outreach Riché will do, because the relationship was already paid for. The risk is that an awkward or generic reconnect breaks the residual trust. The template's job: reconnect with specific shared memory, deliver the now-context in one sentence, and create a low-pressure opening for re-engagement. Done right, dormant reconnects produce more advisory inquiries and warm intros than any other channel.

## How to apply
Structure:
1. **Reconnect with a specific shared memory or experience** — name the cohort, project, conversation, or moment.
2. **Brief update on what Riché is doing now** — one sentence: "I'm building expertise around context architecture in AI products."
3. **One concrete sentence about what that means** — "Here's what I mean: most AI systems skip five critical steps between data and context."
4. **Soft ask** — "If you know anyone wrestling with this problem, I'd love to connect."

Length: 120-180 words. Long enough to re-establish; short enough to read on a phone.

Tone: warm but not overfamiliar. Don't apologize for the silence — it draws attention to it.

## Examples

Example message — to a Techstars cohort founder Riché hasn't spoken to in 18 months:

> Hey [name],
>
> Been thinking about that pivot conversation at Demo Day rehearsal — your line about deciding which audience to ignore has lived in my head for the last 18 months. I keep referring back to it when teams I advise are trying to do everything for everyone.
>
> Quick update on what I'm building now: I'm developing expertise around context architecture in AI products — specifically, the gap between data and useful context that most AI systems skip. The premise: model intelligence is plateauing, and the next moat is in how you shape context before it ever hits the model.
>
> If you know anyone wrestling with retrieval quality, agent reliability, or AI product evals, I'd love to connect. And if there's something on your end you'd want a fresh set of eyes on, I'm always up for that conversation.
>
> Riché

Word count: 145. Specific shared memory (Demo Day rehearsal, the audience-ignore line), one-sentence now-update, concrete frame (data → context gap), soft ask plus reciprocal offer.

## Anti-patterns
- Apologizing for the silence. "Sorry it's been so long" is the most common mistake; it makes the reconnect feel guilt-driven.
- Generic shared memory ("we were in Techstars together"). Must reference a specific moment or conversation.
- A hard ask in the reconnect. Soft ask only — the relationship is being rebuilt, not transacted.
