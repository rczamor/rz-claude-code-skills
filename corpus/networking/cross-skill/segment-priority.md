# Segment Priority

## What it is
A rule for prioritizing who to invest networking time in: use the four audience segments defined in `corpus/growth/segments/` as the lens for every Tier 2 promotion decision and every Tier 1 capacity allocation. Not every senior contact is equal — Segment 1 (CPO/VP of Product at AI-native companies) is the highest-priority audience, and the network should reflect that.

## Why it matters
Networking time is finite. Without a prioritization rule, time gets allocated to whoever happens to be most responsive, which is rarely the highest-leverage audience. Segment priority ensures Riché's Tier 1 capacity (capped at 10) and Tier 2 capacity (~30-50) skew toward the audiences that matter most for his career strategy — speaker invitations, advisory equity, board adjacency.

## How to apply

### The four segments (canonical definitions in `corpus/growth/segments/`)

1. **Segment 1**: CPO / VP of Product at AI-native or AI-forward companies. Highest priority. The audience for Riché's thesis on context architecture in AI products.
2. **Segment 2**: Senior PMs at well-known companies (any industry) interested in AI product craft. Medium-high priority.
3. **Segment 3**: Working PMs at non-AI-native companies trying to ship AI features. Medium priority — large audience but lower per-contact leverage.
4. **Segment 4**: Founders, advisors, investors interested in AI product expertise. Variable priority — context-dependent on whether the relationship maps to advisory or speaker pipelines.

### How to apply across tiers

- **Tier 1 (cap 10)**: target 60-70% Segment 1, 20-30% Segment 4, balance Segment 2.
- **Tier 2 (~30-50)**: target 40-50% Segment 1, 30% Segment 2, 10-20% Segment 4, the rest open.
- **Tier 3**: less curated; let the audience naturally distribute. Tier 3 is volume-driven, not segment-curated.
- **Tier 4 (dormant)**: reactivate Segment 1 and Segment 4 first; the others follow opportunistically.

### What this means in practice

- When a new contact enters the CRM, tag the segment alongside the tier. The segment guides the next-action priority.
- When two Tier 2 candidates both look promising for promotion, the Segment 1 contact takes priority over a Segment 3 contact, all else equal.
- When networking time for the week is tight, allocate first to Segment 1 Tier 2 outreach — that's the highest-ROI work.

## Examples
1. Two Tier 2 candidates earned promotion this week — a Series B AI CPO (Segment 1) and a senior PM at a non-AI bank (Segment 3). With Tier 1 at 9, the CPO promotes; the bank PM stays Tier 2.
2. The weekly review surfaces 8 stale next-actions across Tier 2. Riché prioritizes the 4 Segment 1 entries, executes those, and lets the others wait for next week.

## Anti-patterns
- Treating all senior contacts as equally valuable. They aren't — segment alignment matters more than seniority.
- Letting Segment 3 dominate Tier 2 because they're more responsive. Responsiveness isn't a strategy proxy; segment alignment is.
- Ignoring Segment 4. Founders and investors are often the channel for Segment 1 access; underweighting them is a mistake.
