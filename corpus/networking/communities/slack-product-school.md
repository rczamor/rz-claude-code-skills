# Slack — Product School Slack
## What it is
A broad Slack community attached to Product School's PM courses and certifications. Large, active, free, and skews toward earlier-career PMs — many transitioning into product from other functions or building their first AI product experience. Often-junior audience, with strong volume of beginner-to-intermediate craft questions.

## Why it matters
Product School Slack is Riché's largest Tier 3 outreach surface but his lowest-priority engagement venue per hour spent. The audience is heavily Segment 3 (working PMs at non-AI-native companies), with limited Segment 1 access. The community is useful for content distribution, name-recognition, and occasional unexpected high-value contacts — but the per-message ROI is lower than CPO Club HQ or even MTP.

## How to apply

Engagement style: **broad, often-junior PM audience, limited self-promotion, light cadence.**

1. Pick 1 channel to lurk in (typically #ai-and-ml). Don't try to be in many.
2. Engage selectively — answer 1-2 substantive questions per week where you have a clear, useful frame. Mostly skip role-transition questions and beginner craft questions; let the community handle those.
3. Reference Riché's experience at the right altitude — early-career PMs benefit from concrete Suzy-era examples, not abstract context architecture theory. Lower the abstraction level.
4. Self-promotion: minimal. The community has lower tolerance than MTP for link-drops because the audience is more easily annoyed by senior-PM marketing.
5. The biggest value here is occasional surprise: a senior leader hidden in the community, a fast-rising PM at a Forbes AI 50 company, a rare Segment 1 person who happens to be in the channel. Watch for those signals.

Watch-out: spending more than 2 hours/week in Product School Slack is a sign Riché is in the wrong community for the time. Cap it.

## Examples
1. In #ai-and-ml, a junior PM asks how to evaluate vendor claims about RAG quality. Riché posts a tight, beginner-accessible answer about three things to ask vendors — accessible language, concrete examples, no jargon. Generates broad community appreciation, 1-2 DMs from senior lurkers.
2. A general PM question about transitioning into AI product roles. Riché shares the actual progression he saw at Suzy and the gaps he watched candidates fill. Lots of junior follow-ups; occasionally a senior PM at a target company reaches out as a result.
