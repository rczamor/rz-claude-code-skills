# In-Person Venues

## What it is
The recurring in-person product community venues Riché should be present at — three venues, three different cadences and roles. ProductTank NYC (monthly, local), Lean Product Meetup (virtual, accessible), and PMs of New York via Luma (local NYC PM community). Each has a distinct audience, format, and recommended engagement pattern.

## Why it matters
In-person presence compounds in ways online presence doesn't. Showing up consistently at one venue makes Riché a known regular — which produces unprompted speaker invitations, Tier 2 promotions, and the kind of trust that LinkedIn cannot replicate. The choice of venues matters: ProductTank NYC is Riché's home base, the others are supporting infrastructure.

## How to apply

### ProductTank NYC — monthly, local. **Be a regular.**

- Riché's home meetup. Monthly cadence makes regular attendance plausible.
- Format: typically 2-3 speakers per night, ~150 attendees, mixed seniority with a strong mid-senior PM core.
- Engagement role: regular, occasional speaker. The goal is recognition — when Riché walks in, multiple people know him by name.
- Aim for: attend 9-10 of 12 monthly events per year. Pitch as a speaker once per year minimum, twice if possible.

### Lean Product Meetup — virtual. **Occasional, accessible.**

- Virtual format (Zoom-based) reduces cost-per-attend. Consistent monthly cadence.
- Format: 1-2 speakers, panel-style discussions, ~80 attendees, broad geography.
- Engagement role: occasional. Attend when topic is on-thesis (AI product, context architecture, model-product fit).
- Aim for: attend 4-6 events per year. Speaker pitch viable if topic alignment is strong.

### PMs of New York (Luma) — local NYC PM community. **Occasional.**

- Smaller, more intimate cadence, often around social events plus product discussions.
- Engagement role: occasional. Lower-volume than ProductTank, but more concentrated relationship-building.
- Aim for: attend 3-4 events per year. Better for deepening Tier 2 relationships than building Tier 3.

## Examples
1. ProductTank NYC, monthly attendance: over 12 months, Riché attends 10 events, recognizes ~30 regulars by name, gets pitched as a speaker by the organizers once they've seen him deliver substantive comments and questions multiple times.
2. Lean Product Meetup, occasional: Riché attends a panel specifically on AI product architecture, connects with the panelists, and ends up pitching a follow-up speaking slot the next quarter.
3. PMs of New York: Riché attends a smaller social event, deepens 2 existing Tier 2 relationships through unhurried conversation, and discovers a Tier 3 contact who was previously just a LinkedIn name.

## Anti-patterns
- Trying to be a regular at all three. Splits attention; weakens the ProductTank presence that compounds.
- Skipping the small-format Luma events because they feel low-volume. The relationship density is higher per attendee than at the bigger venues.
- Attending without prep. Even a monthly venue benefits from 30 minutes of speaker-list review.
