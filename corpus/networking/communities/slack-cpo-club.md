# Slack — The CPO Club HQ

## What it is
A curated Slack community for Chief Product Officers and senior product leaders, with 40+ channels covering topics from AI strategy to org design to comp benchmarking. Application-based admission, smaller and higher-quality than the broad PM communities. Direct access to Segment 1 (CPO/VP of Product) per `corpus/growth/segments/`.

## Why it matters
CPO Club HQ is the only Slack community where Riché has direct line-of-sight to his primary audience segment. The signal-to-noise ratio is dramatically higher than MTP or PLA — most messages come from people running real product orgs at companies he wants to be known by. Tier 1 promotions happen here at a rate impossible in the broader communities. The cost: more curated, less self-promotion-tolerant, harder to be visible without earning it through substance.

## How to apply

Engagement style: **direct Segment 1 access, more curated, less self-promotion.**

1. Read significantly more than you post. The community values signal — every post should add something the existing thread didn't have.
2. Pick 1-2 channels to be active in (likely #ai-strategy and #product-leadership). Engage in the rest as a reader, not a poster.
3. When answering, lean on real Riché expertise — Suzy VP of Product context, Grandstage in-flight learnings, advisory pattern observations from Helm Labs. Generic advice gets ignored.
4. Self-promotion: nearly zero. The community will discover Riché's work organically if his comments are substantive. Pushing content backfires.
5. DM matters more than channel posts for relationship building. A great answer in #ai-strategy gets a few likes; the DM follow-up is where the Tier 2 promotion happens.

Watch-out: visibility here is reputational, not transactional. Any sense that Riché is using the community to drive content traffic will accelerate his exit signal.

## Examples
1. In #ai-strategy, a CPO asks how others are evaluating AI product decisions when model improvements are happening every quarter. Riché posts a substantive frame: "Three-tier eval — does this work better than the model alone, will it still work two model generations from now, and does it hold if we swap models?" Generates strong thread engagement; one DM the next day from a Series C CPO leads to a 30-minute call.
2. In #product-leadership, a question about hiring senior PMs into AI product roles. Riché shares the rubric he used at Suzy — specific, behavioral, concrete. Tier 2 promotions follow.

## Anti-patterns
- Treating it like MTP. The cadence and curation expectations are different.
- Posting links to Riché's content. The community has anti-promotion norms stronger than MTP.
- Posting daily. Lower frequency, higher substance is the operating mode.
