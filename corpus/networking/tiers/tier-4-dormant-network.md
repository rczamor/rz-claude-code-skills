# Tier 4 — Dormant Network

## What it is
People who already know Riché — Techstars cohort alumni, Antler portfolio peers, IBM colleagues, Hill Holliday and Phase2 contacts — but haven't been in active contact for months or years. They likely don't know what Riché is doing now. The relationship history exists; the current connection does not. This tier is uniquely valuable because the trust is already there — only the context is stale.

## Why it matters
Dormant networks are the highest-ROI outreach channel because the relationship cost was paid years ago. A reconnect message to a Techstars alum lands at warm-temperature even after silence; the same message to a cold contact would be ignored. Most professionals leave huge value uncashed in dormant networks because they assume reconnect feels awkward — it doesn't, when done right.

## How to apply
1. Audit the major dormant pools: Techstars alumni Slack, Antler portfolio list, IBM LinkedIn connections, Hill Holliday and Phase2 contacts. Estimate count per pool.
2. Tag each dormant contact with the shared context (which cohort, which year, which project). This becomes the reconnect hook.
3. Schedule reconnects in batches — 5-10 per week during the weekly admin block — not all at once.
4. Use the network activation template: shared memory → brief update → concrete frame → soft ask. Never lead with "let's catch up."

## Examples
1. A Techstars cohort founder Riché hasn't spoken to in 18 months. Reconnect: "The pivot conversation we had at Demo Day rehearsal about deciding which audience to ignore — I think about it every week now. Here's where I've ended up." Lands warm because the shared memory is specific.
2. A former IBM design colleague now at a Series B AI company. Riché's reconnect references a specific project they shipped together, then bridges to his context architecture work. The IBM trust shortcuts the credentialing — the conversation starts at substance.

## Anti-patterns
- Mass-blasting dormant networks with a single update. Each message must reference the specific shared context.
- Apologizing for the silence. It draws attention to it and frames the reconnect as guilt-driven instead of value-driven.
