# Tier 2 — Active Network

## What it is
Thirty to fifty people Riché engages with regularly through LinkedIn comments, occasional DMs, and event encounters. They know Riché's thesis on context architecture, they would refer him without hesitation, and they expect to see his work in their feed. This tier is maintained through consistent content engagement plus periodic direct contact — not through every-week effort, but through every-week visibility.

## Why it matters
Tier 2 is the layer that converts Riché's content into opportunities. Inner Circle is too small to surface most opportunities; Extended Network doesn't know him well enough to refer him. Tier 2 is where speaker invitations, advisory requests, and warm intros to Tier 1 actually originate. A healthy Tier 2 means inbound flow — when it goes quiet, the pipeline goes quiet.

## How to apply
1. Maintain a list of 30-50 people. Comment substantively on at least 5 of their posts per week.
2. DM 1-2 Tier 2 contacts per week with something specific — a relevant article, a reaction to their work, an intro offer.
3. Promote Tier 2 → Tier 1 when a relationship deepens past three substantive exchanges; demote to Tier 3 when six months pass with no two-way engagement.
4. Use the weekly admin block to scan Tier 2 activity and queue the next round of touches.

## Examples
1. A product leader at a mid-stage AI startup who regularly reposts Riché's context architecture pieces; Riché reciprocates with substantive comments on their launch posts. Eventually invites Riché to speak at their internal product offsite.
2. A Lenny's Newsletter community contributor whose long posts on PM craft consistently get sharp replies from Riché. Months later, they DM him about an advisory inquiry.

## Anti-patterns
- Confusing volume with value: 50 surface-level comments per week build no Tier 2.
- Letting Tier 2 collapse to Tier 1's audience. They need their own attention pattern, not crumbs from Tier 1 maintenance.
