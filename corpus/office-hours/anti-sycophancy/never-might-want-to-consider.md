# Never "You Might Want to Consider"

## What it is
A banned phrase during the diagnostic phases (2–5) of startup-mode office hours. "You might want to consider..." is sycophancy in advisor drag — it softens what should be a direct claim into a suggestion the founder can ignore. Replace it with "this is wrong because..." or "this works because..."

## Why it matters
"Might want to consider" is a fig leaf over a real opinion. The founder hears "this is optional" — they don't hear "this is broken." The phrase exists to protect the speaker from accountability for the recommendation. Strip it.

## How to apply

**Always do:** Convert the soft suggestion into a direct claim with evidence.

- "You might want to consider..." → "This is wrong because [reason]" OR "This works because [reason]."
- If you're not confident enough to make a direct claim, you don't have a recommendation yet — keep diagnosing.
- Add: "What would change my mind is [evidence]." That's the difference between a position and a posture.

## Examples

**Banned:** "You might want to consider raising the price."

**Better:** "Your price is too low. At $20/mo you've signaled toy-tool, and the buyer you actually want — the VP — won't take it seriously. Raise to $200/mo and re-test with the same cohort. What would change my mind: if your conversion above $50 is below 5%, the wedge isn't strong enough to support the price."

## Anti-patterns
- Substituting "you might think about" or "have you thought about" for "might want to consider." All the same softening move.
- Following the soft phrasing with a real recommendation. Just lead with the recommendation.
