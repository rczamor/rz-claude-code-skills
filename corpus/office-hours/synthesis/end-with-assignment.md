# End with the Assignment

## What it is
A synthesis template for closing every office-hours session. Every session should produce one concrete thing the founder should do next. Not a strategy — an action. The assignment is the deliverable; everything before it was diagnosis.

## Why it matters
Sessions that end with strategy advice produce no behavior change. Sessions that end with one specific action — "watch one user use the product for an hour without helping them, this week, send me what surprised you" — produce work the founder can do, fail at, learn from, and bring back. The assignment is what makes the session compound.

## How to apply

- Pick one thing. Not three. The assignment is singular.
- Make it an *action*, not a *strategy* or a *decision*. Strategy is hard to verify; action is observable.
- Time-box it: this week, by Friday, before our next session.
- Match it to the diagnostic gap surfaced during the session. If the founder couldn't name a user, the assignment is observation. If they couldn't articulate a wedge, the assignment is to write one paragraph describing the wedge and what it costs.
- Switch warmth back on for the close — during the diagnostic, take a position; for the assignment, deliver it with care.

## Examples

1. **Diagnostic gap was demand reality:** "Assignment: by Friday, find one person who currently spends 5+ hours a week on this exact problem and watch them do it for 30 minutes without helping. Send me what surprised you."
2. **Diagnostic gap was wedge sharpness:** "Assignment: this week, write one paragraph that describes the smallest version of this someone would pay $200/mo for. If you can't write the paragraph in 200 words or less, the wedge isn't sharp enough."
3. **Diagnostic gap was named human:** "Assignment: by next session, name three real people — names, titles, companies, and one thing each said to you in their own words. If you can't, the next session is going to be a repeat of this one."

## Anti-patterns
- Ending with strategy advice ("focus on positioning"). Strategy isn't an assignment — it's a topic.
- Giving three assignments. The founder will do zero. One is the discipline.
- Ending without an assignment. The session was diagnosis without prescription — incomplete.
