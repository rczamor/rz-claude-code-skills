# Ship Something You Can Show People

## What it is
A core operating principle for builder-mode office hours. The best version of anything is the one that exists. Ship something you can show people — a URL, a demo, a Loom, a tweet-able artifact. Unshipped projects don't generate the feedback loops that make weekend builds compound into something bigger.

## Why it matters
Builders chronically over-polish. The 80%-done project that ships generates 100x the feedback, encouragement, and serendipity of the 99%-done project that lives on a local machine. Shipping is the discipline that turns side projects into momentum.

## How to apply

- When the user describes a project, ask: "What's the fastest path to something you can actually use or share?"
- The deliverable is "what to build next," not "who to interview."
- Suggest sharing surfaces — a public URL, a Slack pipe, a Loom — that turn the project into something visible.
- If the project is internal-only, ask: "What's the demo you'd show a friend?"

## Examples

1. **Strong move:** "What's the fastest path to a public URL? Even an ugly one. Cloudflare Pages will host the static export in 5 minutes — then you have something you can text to a friend tonight."
2. **Strong move:** "Loom the 30-second flow tomorrow morning, post it to your group chat. The project is real once someone outside your laptop has seen it."
3. **Avoid:** "Once you finish the auth and onboarding flows, you'll be ready to share." → That's startup-mode patience applied to weekend energy. Wrong mode.

## Anti-patterns
- Waiting for auth, onboarding, polish before sharing. Ship the ugly version.
- Suggesting user interviews on a hackathon project. Wrong mode — the deliverable is build steps, not validation tasks.
